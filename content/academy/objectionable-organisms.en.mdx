# Objectionable Organisms in Non-Sterile Products

For non-sterile products, passing the USP 61/62 microbial limits is not the whole
story. An organism can be within total count limits yet still be
**objectionable** — unacceptable given the product, its use, and the patient.

## What makes an organism objectionable?

It depends on context, not just a list:

- **Route of administration** — an organism tolerable in a topical may be
  dangerous in an inhalation or pediatric oral product.
- **Patient population** — immunocompromised users raise the risk.
- **Product chemistry** — whether the formulation supports proliferation.

## Beyond the specified organisms

USP 62 names specific organisms to test for, but the absence of those does not
prove a product is safe. A documented **microbial risk assessment** is what
auditors expect to see justifying which organisms matter for your product.
