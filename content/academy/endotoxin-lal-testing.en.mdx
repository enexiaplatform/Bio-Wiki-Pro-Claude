# Bacterial Endotoxins — LAL Testing

Bacterial endotoxins (lipopolysaccharide from Gram-negative cell walls) are
pyrogenic and must be controlled in parenteral products and medical devices.
Testing follows **USP &lt;85&gt;** / **EP 2.6.14**.

## Methods

- **Gel-clot** — qualitative/semi-quantitative; the historical reference method.
- **Kinetic turbidimetric** — measures turbidity development over time.
- **Kinetic chromogenic** — measures colour from a synthetic substrate.

## The endotoxin limit

The limit is **K / M**, where:

- **K** = threshold pyrogenic dose (e.g. 5 EU/kg for most parenterals; 0.2 EU/kg intrathecal)
- **M** = maximum dose per kg per hour

## Validation essentials

Every product must pass **interference testing** (inhibition/enhancement) to
prove the matrix doesn't distort recovery — typically demonstrated by spike
recovery within 50–200%.
