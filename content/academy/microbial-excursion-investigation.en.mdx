# Investigating Microbial Excursions (EM Data)

An environmental-monitoring (EM) excursion is not automatically a disaster — but
how you investigate it tells an auditor whether your contamination control is
real. The goal: understand the event, assess product impact, and prevent
recurrence.

## Alert vs action response

- **Alert level** — an early-warning signal. Heighten scrutiny; investigate if it
  recurs or trends. Not necessarily a formal investigation on its own.
- **Action level** — exceeds the acceptable threshold. Triggers a **formal
  investigation** with root cause and CAPA, and a product-impact assessment.

## What a credible investigation covers

1. **Confirm the result** — rule out lab/sampling error (without "testing into
   compliance").
2. **Identify the organism** — to at least genus/species; the ID often points to
   the source (skin flora → gowning/technique; Gram-negative → water/spills;
   mould → construction/HVAC).
3. **Correlate with trends** — same location, same organism, same shift?
4. **Assess product impact** — what was exposed between the last clean result and
   the excursion, and is the batch's sterility/quality assured?

## The classic failure

Closing an action-level excursion as "isolated event, no impact" without an
organism ID, trend review, or impact rationale. Auditors read that as a system
that doesn't learn.
