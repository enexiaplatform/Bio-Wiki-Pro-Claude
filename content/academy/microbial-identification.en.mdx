# Microbial Identification Methods

When monitoring detects an organism, identification tells you *what* it is and
*where* it likely came from — essential for investigations and CCS.

## Phenotypic vs genotypic

- **Phenotypic:** Gram stain, biochemical tests, automated systems, **MALDI-TOF**
  (protein mass fingerprint — fast, widely used).
- **Genotypic:** **16S rRNA sequencing** (bacteria), ITS (fungi) — the reference
  standard, used when phenotypic results are ambiguous.

## When ID is required

- Grade A/B environmental excursions
- Sterility test positives
- Objectionable organisms in non-sterile products
- Building an isolate library for the contamination control strategy

## Practical note

A characterised in-house isolate library makes investigations far faster —
matching an excursion to a known house flora pinpoints the likely source.
