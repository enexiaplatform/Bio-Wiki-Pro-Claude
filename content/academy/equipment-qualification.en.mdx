# Equipment Qualification (IQ/OQ/PQ)

Qualification provides documented evidence that equipment is suitable for its
intended use and performs reliably. It is the equipment-level counterpart to
process validation and follows a staged sequence.

## The qualification stages

- **DQ — Design Qualification** — the design meets the User Requirements
  Specification (URS) and GMP needs.
- **IQ — Installation Qualification** — installed correctly: utilities,
  calibration, documentation, and components per spec.
- **OQ — Operational Qualification** — performs across its full operating
  ranges, including alarms and interlocks.
- **PQ — Performance Qualification** — performs consistently under real,
  routine production conditions.

## The URS anchors everything

A clear URS defines acceptance criteria up front and gives every later stage
something objective to test against. Weak URS = ambiguous qualification.

## Qualification feeds validation

PQ results feed directly into process validation and the control strategy.
Requalification is triggered by change control, periodic review, or performance
drift detected in trending.
