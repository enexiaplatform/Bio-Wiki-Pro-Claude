# Internal Audits & Self-Inspection

GMP requires manufacturers to **inspect themselves**. A self-inspection (internal
audit) program checks that the quality system is actually implemented and
effective — and finds problems before a regulator or customer does.

## Risk-based scheduling

Audit areas on a defined cycle, weighted by **risk and history** — sterile
operations, data integrity, and high-deviation areas more often than low-risk
ones. Cover the whole quality system over a planned period (often annually).

## Independence and objectivity

Auditors should be **independent of the area** they audit — you can't objectively
audit your own work. Auditors are trained, and findings rest on **objective
evidence** (records, observations), not opinion.

## From finding to improvement

A finding is only useful if it drives action:

- Classify by severity (critical / major / minor).
- Root-cause the systemic issues (not just the symptom).
- **CAPA** with owners and due dates, then **verify effectiveness**.
- Trend findings across audits to spot recurring weaknesses.

## Management review link

Self-inspection results feed **management review** (ICH Q10) — leadership sees the
real state of compliance and allocates resources. An audit program that never
escalates anything is itself a red flag.

> Tip: the best internal audits are slightly uncomfortable. A program that only
> ever finds minor issues usually isn't looking hard enough.
