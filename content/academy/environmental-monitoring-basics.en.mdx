# Environmental Monitoring — Foundations

Environmental Monitoring (EM) is the routine measurement of microbial and
particulate contamination in classified areas. It is a core pillar of a
Contamination Control Strategy (CCS) under EU GMP Annex 1 (2022).

## What EM measures

- **Viable monitoring** — settle plates, active air sampling, surface contact
  plates/swabs, and personnel (glove/gown) monitoring.
- **Non-viable monitoring** — airborne particle counts (≥0.5 µm and ≥5.0 µm).

## Alert vs action limits

| Limit | Meaning | Typical response |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Alert | Early warning; trend signal | Increase scrutiny, investigate if recurring |
| Action | Exceeds acceptable level | Formal investigation + CAPA |

## Why trending matters

A single excursion rarely tells the story — **trends do**. Recurring alert-level
results in the same location often precede an action-level failure. A mature EM
program reviews trends per location and per organism, not just pass/fail.

> Tip: map your sampling locations to risk (proximity to the product/Grade A),
> not convenience. Auditors expect a risk-based rationale for every site.
