# Out-of-Trend (OOT) Investigation

An Out-of-Trend result is **within specification** but deviates from the
expected historical pattern. OOT detection catches problems early — before they
become an OOS failure or a recall.

## Three types of trend signal

1. **Within-batch trend** — a single result drifts from prior data points.
2. **Across-batch trend** — successive batches creep in one direction.
3. **Stability trend** — a stability time point falls off the projected line.

## Detecting OOT

Use statistical control limits (e.g. mean ± 2–3 SD) or regression-based limits
from historical data — not the spec limits. A result inside spec but outside the
control limit is an OOT signal.

## Setting OOT limits

Build limits from historical data, separate from the spec:

- **Control-chart limits** — mean ± 2 SD (alert) and ± 3 SD (action) from a stable
  baseline of prior batches.
- **Regression limits (stability)** — a confidence/prediction interval around the
  fitted degradation line; a point outside it is an OOT even if within spec.
- **By-the-firm rules** — e.g. the slope-control or 3-of-last-results approaches
  used for stability trending.

Pick the method before you need it, document it, and apply it consistently — a
limit invented after a surprising result isn't a control.

## OOT decision tree

1. **Confirm the data** — is the value real (no transcription/calc error)?
2. **Classify the signal** — within-batch, across-batch, or stability trend.
3. **Assess impact** — does the trend threaten the spec within the product's life
   or shelf life? Project it forward.
4. **Investigate the cause** — method drift, instrument, raw-material shift,
   process change, or genuine product change.
5. **Act** — document, and if systemic or impactful, raise a CAPA; for stability,
   assess whether already-released batches are affected.

## Linking OOT to APR and CAPA

OOT signals are a primary input to the **Annual Product Review** — the APR is
where slow across-batch drifts become visible and where the effectiveness of
prior CAPAs is judged. A documented OOT that never feeds the APR or a CAPA is a
detection system with no response attached.

> Practitioner tip: the value of OOT monitoring is *lead time*. A trend caught at
> 2 SD gives you weeks to investigate calmly; the same problem caught as an OOS
> gives you a batch failure and a clock. Invest in the trending, not just the spec
> check.
