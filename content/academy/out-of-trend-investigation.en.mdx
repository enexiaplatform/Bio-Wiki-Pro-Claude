# Out-of-Trend (OOT) Investigation

An Out-of-Trend result is **within specification** but deviates from the
expected historical pattern. OOT detection catches problems early — before they
become an OOS failure or a recall.

## Three types of trend signal

1. **Within-batch trend** — a single result drifts from prior data points.
2. **Across-batch trend** — successive batches creep in one direction.
3. **Stability trend** — a stability time point falls off the projected line.

## Detecting OOT

Use statistical control limits (e.g. mean ± 2–3 SD) or regression-based limits
from historical data — not the spec limits. A result inside spec but outside the
control limit is an OOT signal.
