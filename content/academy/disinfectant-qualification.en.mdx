# Disinfectant Efficacy & Cleanroom Disinfection

Cleanroom disinfection is a core element of the Contamination Control Strategy.
EU GMP Annex 1 expects disinfectants to be **qualified** — proven to work on your
surfaces, organisms, and contact times — not just purchased and sprayed.

## What efficacy qualification proves

- **Surface efficacy** — the required log reduction against representative
  organisms (including environmental isolates) on each material type (stainless
  steel, glass, polymers, vinyl).
- **Contact (wet) time** — the surface must stay wet for the validated duration;
  "wipe and dry instantly" doesn't disinfect.
- **In-use shelf life** — how long a prepared/diluted solution stays effective.

## Rotation and sporicides

Routine disinfectants (e.g. quaternary ammonium, alcohols) may not kill **spores**.
A program therefore rotates in a **sporicidal agent** (e.g. peroxide- or
hypochlorite-based) periodically. Rotation is risk-based — documented rationale,
not arbitrary alternation.

## Aseptic-area requirements

In Grade A/B areas, disinfectants are **sterile** (or sterile-filtered at point of
use), within expiry, and applied per a validated technique. Disinfectant residues
and their removal are also considered.

> Tip: include your own **environmental isolates** in efficacy testing — a
> disinfectant that beats lab strains but not your house flora gives false comfort.
