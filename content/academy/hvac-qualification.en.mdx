# HVAC Qualification for Cleanrooms

The HVAC system is the engine of a cleanroom. Qualifying it demonstrates that
filtration, airflow, pressure, and environmental control reliably maintain the
required classification per EU GMP Annex 1 and ISO 14644.

## What HVAC qualification proves

- **HEPA filter integrity** — filters are installed and leak-free (DOP/PAO test).
- **Air change rate** — enough air volume to dilute and remove contamination.
- **Airflow pattern** — unidirectional flow where required (smoke studies in
  Grade A).
- **Pressure cascade** — cleaner rooms held at higher pressure so air flows
  outward, protecting the critical zone.
- **Temperature & humidity** — within ranges for product and personnel.

## Recovery time

A key performance test: after a defined disturbance, how long the room takes to
return to its classified particle level. Annex 1 expects a documented,
risk-based recovery performance for the grade.

## Tying it together

HVAC qualification feeds cleanroom classification and the Contamination Control
Strategy. Requalification is triggered periodically and after changes to filters,
ductwork, room layout, or air balancing.

> Tip: pressure differentials and air changes drift over time as filters load —
> trend them continuously, don't just re-test once a year.
