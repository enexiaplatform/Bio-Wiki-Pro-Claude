# Acceptance Sampling & AQL (ANSI/ASQ Z1.4)

Acceptance sampling lets you make a release/reject decision about a lot by
inspecting a sample instead of 100% of units. ANSI/ASQ Z1.4 is the standard plan;
it is widely used for visual inspection and attribute defects.

## How a Z1.4 plan is built

1. **Lot size + inspection level** → a **sample size code letter**.
2. **Code letter + AQL** → a **sample size** and **accept (Ac) / reject (Re)**
   numbers.
3. Inspect the sample: defects ≤ Ac → accept; defects ≥ Re → reject.

## AQL is a risk decision

The **Acceptable Quality Limit** is the worst tolerable process average that the
plan will routinely accept — a lower AQL is stricter. Defects are usually
classified (critical / major / minor), each with its own AQL, because a critical
defect carries far more risk than a cosmetic one.

## Switching rules

Z1.4 has **normal → tightened → reduced** switching rules: consistent good
quality earns reduced inspection; failures trigger tightened inspection. Skipping
these rules is a common audit finding.

> Tip: a sampling plan does not "prove" a lot is defect-free — it bounds the
> *risk* of accepting a bad lot. Document the rationale for the AQLs you choose.
