# Analytical Procedure Lifecycle (ICH Q14)

ICH Q14 brings **Quality-by-Design (QbD)** thinking to analytical methods. Instead
of treating a method as a fixed recipe that gets validated once, it frames methods
as having a **lifecycle**: design → performance qualification → ongoing
monitoring — mirroring the process-validation lifecycle.

## The Analytical Target Profile (ATP)

The ATP is the method's "what good looks like" — the performance requirements
(accuracy, precision, range, specificity) the procedure must meet to be fit for
its purpose, defined **before** development. It anchors everything downstream,
much like CQAs anchor process design.

## Minimal vs enhanced approach

- **Minimal** — a traditional, fixed method developed and validated to Q2.
- **Enhanced (QbD)** — systematically explore method parameters to understand
  their effect on performance, define a **Method Operable Design Region (MODR)**,
  and build a control strategy. Within the MODR, adjustments aren't a change to the
  registered method.

## How it complements Q2(R2)

Q14 (development) and **Q2(R2)** (validation) are companion guidelines: Q14 covers
how you develop and describe the method and its robustness; Q2 covers the
validation characteristics and how they're demonstrated.

## Why it matters

The enhanced approach gives **regulatory flexibility** (changes within the MODR
without prior approval) and methods that are robust by design — fewer OOS caused
by the method itself.
