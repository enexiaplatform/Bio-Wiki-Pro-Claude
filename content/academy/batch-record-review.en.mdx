# Batch Record Review & Product Release

Batch record review is the final quality gate before product reaches patients. QA
confirms the batch was made and tested per the approved process and that every
deviation was resolved before disposition.

## What the review confirms

- The **executed batch record** is complete, with no missing entries, unexplained
  gaps, or uncorrected errors (good documentation practice throughout).
- In-process and finished-product results meet specification.
- All deviations, OOS/OOT results, and change controls affecting the batch are
  closed with an impact assessment.
- Reconciliation (yield, labels, components) is within limits.

## Review by exception

In mature, electronic systems, "review by exception" focuses human attention on
flagged anomalies rather than re-checking every in-spec value — but it depends on
a validated system and a sound risk basis.

## The disposition decision

Release is a documented decision: **release, reject, or hold/quarantine**. In the
EU, a **Qualified Person (QP)** certifies each batch and carries personal legal
responsibility for it.

## Batch-record-review checklist

- [ ] Executed record matches the approved master/MBR version.
- [ ] All steps signed/dated by performer and verifier where required; no blanks,
      no undocumented corrections (single-line, initialed, dated).
- [ ] In-process checks within limits; finished-product results meet spec.
- [ ] Yield and reconciliation (components, labels) within defined ranges.
- [ ] Every deviation, OOS/OOT, and change control affecting the batch is closed
      with an impact assessment concluding "no adverse impact" (or justified).
- [ ] Environmental/utility data for the manufacturing period acceptable.
- [ ] Stability commitments and any post-approval conditions met.
- [ ] Certificate of Analysis accurate and traceable to raw data.

## Deviation impact assessment — the framework

For each open item ask: **What** happened, **what batch attribute** could it
affect, **what data** addresses that concern, and **what is the conclusion**?
Disposition cannot proceed until each deviation has a documented impact call. A
deviation marked "closed" with no impact statement is a release that won't survive
audit.

## Parametric release

For terminally sterilized products, **parametric release** lets validated process
data (e.g. the full sterilization F0 record) substitute for the finished-product
sterility test — but only with regulatory approval, a robust CCS, and tight
control of the critical parameters. It raises, not lowers, the bar on data
integrity for those parameters.

> Practitioner tip: release decisions should be reproducible by a second reviewer
> from the record alone. If the reasoning lives in someone's head, the record is
> incomplete — write down *why* the batch is releasable, not just that it is.
