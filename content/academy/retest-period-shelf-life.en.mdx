# Retest Period & Shelf-Life Assignment (ICH Q1E)

Stability data exist to answer one question: how long can this material be used?
ICH Q1E describes how to evaluate that data and assign a **retest period** (drug
substances) or a **shelf life / expiry** (drug products).

> Practice it: use the free [Stability Trend & Shelf-Life Planner](/tools/stability-trend-shelf-life-planner)
> to compare the proposed period with real-time data, projected headroom, and
> the nearest specification limit.

## Retest period vs shelf life

- **Retest period** — for a **drug substance**: after this date, the material can
  still be used **if it is retested** against specification and conforms.
- **Shelf life (expiry)** — for a **drug product**: a hard date after which the
  product must not be used.

The distinction matters: an expired *product* is gone, but a drug substance past
its retest date is salvageable pending a passing retest.

## Evaluating the data

You assess whether attributes change over time. If an attribute changes with time,
you analyze the trend (often regression) and find where the limit is crossed. The
proposed period is supported by the long-term data at the recommended storage
condition.

## Extrapolation — within limits

ICH Q1E permits **limited extrapolation** beyond the period covered by real-time
data — but only when the data show little change and low variability, with
statistical justification. The more change you see, the less you may extrapolate.

> Tip: extrapolation is a privilege, not a default. A "little to no change with
> tight data" profile earns it; a drifting attribute does not.
