# Pharmaceutical Water Systems — Monitoring

Water is the most-used raw material in pharma manufacturing, so its quality is
continuously controlled. The two key grades are **Purified Water (PW)** and
**Water for Injection (WFI)**.

## Key attributes

| Attribute | Why it matters |
| --- | --- |
| Conductivity | Detects ionic contamination (USP &lt;645&gt;) |
| Total Organic Carbon (TOC) | Detects organic contamination (USP &lt;643&gt;) |
| Microbial count | Bioburden control |
| Endotoxin (WFI) | Pyrogen control |

## Microbial limits (guidance)

- **PW:** action limit 100 CFU/mL
- **WFI:** action limit 10 CFU/100 mL + endotoxin &lt; 0.25 EU/mL

## Monitoring philosophy

Water systems are monitored on a **risk-based sampling plan** covering source,
generation, storage, and each point of use. Trends matter more than single
results — a slow upward drift in counts signals biofilm before any limit breach.
