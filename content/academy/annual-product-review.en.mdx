# Annual Product Review (APR / PQR)

The Annual Product Review (US, 21 CFR 211.180(e)) and Product Quality Review (EU
GMP Chapter 1) is a periodic, documented evaluation of a product's manufacturing
data to verify process consistency and the appropriateness of current
specifications.

## What goes in

- Batch data and in-process results (with trending).
- Deviations, OOS/OOT results, and their investigations.
- Change controls and CAPAs.
- Complaints, returns, and recalls.
- Stability data and any specification changes.
- Qualification status of relevant equipment and utilities.

## Turning it into a real tool

A common finding is the **"copy-paste" APR** — last year's report with new dates.
A meaningful review *trends across batches*, asks whether the process is still in
a state of control, and feeds conclusions back into the control strategy.

## Timing and ownership

Define a review period and a due date, assign an owner, and route conclusions
through QA. Recurring issues identified in the review should generate CAPAs, not
just a sign-off.

> Tip: link the APR to your trending program so the data assembles itself.
> Reviews built from a living dataset beat ones reconstructed once a year.
