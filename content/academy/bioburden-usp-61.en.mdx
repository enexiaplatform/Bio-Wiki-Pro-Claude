# Bioburden & Microbial Limits (USP &lt;61&gt;/&lt;62&gt;)

For non-sterile products, microbial limits testing controls the microbial
quality of the product. It has two parts under **USP &lt;61&gt;** and
**USP &lt;62&gt;**.

## The two tests

- **USP &lt;61&gt; — Microbial Enumeration:** counts total aerobic microbial
  count (**TAMC**) and total yeast & mould count (**TYMC**).
- **USP &lt;62&gt; — Tests for Specified Microorganisms:** detects objectionable
  organisms (e.g. *E. coli*, *S. aureus*, *P. aeruginosa*, *Salmonella*).

## Acceptance criteria

Limits depend on the route of administration and dosage form (see USP &lt;1111&gt;).
Oral non-aqueous products, for example, have different limits from topicals.

## Method suitability

Before routine use, demonstrate the method recovers low inocula (typically
10–100 CFU) of standard challenge organisms within a factor of 2 — proving the
product matrix doesn't inhibit recovery.
