# Cleaning Validation

Cleaning validation documents that a cleaning procedure consistently reduces
residues (product, degradants, cleaning agents, bioburden) below safe limits —
preventing cross-contamination in shared equipment.

## Acceptance limits

Modern limits are based on **Health-Based Exposure Limits (HBEL / PDE)** per the
EMA guideline, rather than the older 1/1000-dose and 10-ppm criteria alone.

## Sampling methods

- **Swab sampling** — direct, for hard-to-reach "worst-case" locations.
- **Rinse sampling** — covers large surface areas indirectly.

## Worst-case logic

Validate the hardest-to-clean product, equipment train, and locations. A
successful worst case justifies a grouping/bracketing approach.

## MACO calculation — worked example

**Maximum Allowable Carryover (MACO)** is the most product-A residue that may
safely carry into the next product B. The HBEL-based formula:

```
MACO = (PDE_A × MBS_B) / MDD_B
```

where PDE_A = permitted daily exposure of product A (mg/day), MBS_B = minimum
batch size of B (mg), MDD_B = maximum daily dose of B (mg/day).

*Example:* PDE_A = 0.1 mg/day, MBS_B = 50,000,000 mg (50 kg), MDD_B = 1,000
mg/day → MACO = (0.1 × 50,000,000) / 1,000 = **5,000 mg** of A across the whole
batch of B. Divide by shared surface area to get the limit **per cm²**, then by
the swab area to get the per-swab acceptance value the lab reports against.

## Recovery study (the step people skip)

Swab and rinse results are only valid after a **recovery study**: spike a coupon
of the actual surface material (316L steel, PTFE, glass) with a known residue
level, then sample and recover it. A recovery factor below ~70–80% must be
justified or the method improved — and results are **corrected** by the recovery
factor. No recovery data = uninterpretable cleaning results.

## Acceptance criteria you must define up front

1. **Chemical** — residue ≤ MACO-derived per-area limit (corrected for recovery).
2. **Cleaning agent** — detergent residue below its own HBEL/toxicity limit.
3. **Microbial** — bioburden (and endotoxin for sterile) below action levels.
4. **Visual** — visually clean at a defined viewing distance/lux (necessary but
   never sufficient on its own).

## Campaign length and hold times

- **Dirty hold time (DHT)** — max time soiled equipment may sit before cleaning.
- **Clean hold time (CHT)** — max time clean equipment stays clean before use.
- **Campaign** — number of consecutive batches before a full clean; validate the
  worst-case (longest) campaign, then monitor.

Both DHT and CHT must be validated with microbial data, not assumed.

> Practitioner tip: build a residue/equipment matrix and validate the single
> hardest-to-clean product on the hardest-to-clean equipment train. A successful
> worst case lets you bracket the rest instead of validating every combination.
