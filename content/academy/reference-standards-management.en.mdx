# Reference Standards Management

Reference standards are the measuring stick for every quantitative test. If the
standard is wrong, every result calibrated against it is wrong — so their
qualification, traceability, and handling are core lab controls.

## Primary vs secondary

- **Primary (official) standard** — sourced from a pharmacopoeia (USP, EP, etc.)
  or a manufacturer's primary lot; its value is established by definitive methods.
- **Secondary (working) standard** — an in-house standard **qualified against the
  primary** so routine testing doesn't consume expensive primary material.

Each working standard must carry an assigned value with documented traceability
back to the primary.

## What auditors look for

- A **register**: identity, lot, source, assigned value/potency, expiry/re-test
  date, and storage conditions.
- **Qualification records** for every secondary standard.
- **Storage monitoring** at the defined conditions, with controlled access.
- Use within the assigned **re-test / expiry** date.

## Common pitfalls

- Using a working standard past its re-test date.
- No traceability from the working standard back to a primary.
- Storing light- or moisture-sensitive standards without monitoring.

> Tip: treat reference standards like calibrated instruments — qualified,
> tracked, stored to spec, and re-qualified on a schedule.
