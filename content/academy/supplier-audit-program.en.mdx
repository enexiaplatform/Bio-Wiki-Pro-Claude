# Supplier Audit Programs

Supplier qualification doesn't end at approval — ongoing **audits** verify that a
supplier keeps meeting requirements. A mature program is risk-based, not
one-size-fits-all.

## Audit types by assurance level

- **Questionnaire / desk audit** — lowest effort; suitable for low-risk materials.
- **Remote / virtual audit** — document and process review over video; gained
  acceptance post-2020.
- **On-site audit** — strongest assurance; expected for high-risk suppliers (APIs,
  sterile components, sole-source critical materials).

## Frequency follows risk

Audit interval should reflect material criticality and supplier performance — a
critical API maker might be audited every 1–3 years and after major changes or
quality issues, while a low-risk commodity supplier may rely on questionnaires.
A fixed "everyone every 3 years" schedule ignoring risk is a common finding.

## Closing the loop

Findings must drive action: CAPA from the supplier, re-evaluation of approved
status, and trending across audits. An audit report that's filed and forgotten
provides assurance on paper only.

> Tip: tie the audit program to your supplier risk assessment and change control,
> so a supplier change or a quality signal can re-trigger an audit ahead of schedule.
