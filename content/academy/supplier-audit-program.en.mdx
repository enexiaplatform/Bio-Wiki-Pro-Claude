# Supplier Audit Programs

Supplier qualification does not end at approval. Audits check whether the
supplier's quality system, process controls, data integrity, material controls,
and change-management practices still support the approved status.

## Audit types by assurance level

- **Questionnaire or desk assessment** - useful for low-risk suppliers or as a
  first screen before deeper review.
- **Remote audit** - useful for document review, quality-system interviews, CAPA
  follow-up, and lower-risk or travel-limited situations.
- **On-site audit** - strongest assurance for high-risk suppliers such as API
  manufacturers, sterile component suppliers, contract sterilizers, critical test
  labs, or sole-source suppliers.

The audit type should match risk. A fixed approach for every supplier wastes
effort on low-risk suppliers and under-controls high-risk suppliers.

## Frequency follows risk

Audit interval should reflect:

- material or service criticality;
- supplier performance history;
- previous audit findings;
- regulatory inspection status;
- change-notification reliability;
- complaint, deviation, or incoming-test trends;
- sole-source or supply-continuity risk.

A high-risk supplier may need audit every one to three years and after major
changes or quality issues. A low-risk supplier may be managed through
questionnaire and periodic documentation review.

## Closing the loop

An audit report is not the end of the audit. Findings should drive supplier CAPA,
approved-supplier status decisions, increased incoming verification where
justified, and follow-up to confirm effectiveness.

Strong programs define:

- finding classification and due dates;
- evidence required for closure;
- escalation when CAPA is late or weak;
- triggers for probation, restricted approval, or disqualification;
- linkage to supplier performance review and management review.

> Tip: tie the audit program to supplier risk assessment and change control, so a
> supplier change, complaint trend, or quality signal can trigger an audit ahead
> of schedule.
