# Contamination Control Strategy (CCS)

The 2022 revision of EU GMP Annex 1 makes the **Contamination Control Strategy**
mandatory: a documented, holistic, site-wide plan describing how all
contamination controls work together to assure product sterility.

## What a CCS must integrate

- Facility & equipment design (flows, segregation, HVAC)
- Personnel and gowning
- Utilities (water, gases, clean steam)
- Environmental & process monitoring
- Cleaning, disinfection, and sterilization
- Process and aseptic-technique controls

## Risk-based, not a checklist

A CCS is not a binder — it's a living risk assessment. Each control is justified
against the contamination risks it mitigates, with effectiveness reviewed using
real monitoring data.

## The CCS element map

Annex 1 (§2.3) lists the elements your CCS must address. A practical way to
structure it is source → control → monitor:

| Contamination source | Primary controls | Monitoring evidence |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Personnel | Gowning qual, behavior, limits on numbers | Glove/gown EM, APS results |
| Air / HVAC | Grade A–D classification, pressure cascade, HEPA | Viable/non-viable EM, DP trends |
| Surfaces | Cleaning & disinfection, sporicidal rotation | Surface EM, disinfectant efficacy |
| Water & gases | WFI/PW system design, point-of-use filters | TOC, conductivity, bioburden, endotoxin |
| Process / equipment | Closed systems, RABS/isolator, SIP/CIP | APS, integrity tests, bioburden |
| Raw materials / components | Supplier control, bioburden limits, depyro | CoA, incoming bioburden/endotoxin |

## Gap-assessment approach

1. List each Annex 1 element above.
2. Map the existing control(s) and the data that proves they work.
3. Rate residual risk (with current controls) using QRM (ICH Q9).
4. Flag gaps — element with no control, or a control with no monitoring data.
5. Feed gaps into CAPA / continuous-improvement with owners and dates.

The output is a single, defensible document showing controls are not isolated
but **interact** — e.g. weaker gowning is offset by an isolator, or a manual step
is justified by intensive monitoring.

## How inspectors probe the CCS

Expect questions that test whether the CCS is real or decorative: "Show me how a
recent EM excursion fed back into the CCS." "Where is the link between this APS
failure and your control changes?" "Which controls are you relying on most, and
what's your evidence they're effective?" A CCS that can't connect a real event to
a control adjustment reads as a paperwork exercise.

> Practitioner tip: keep the CCS owned by a cross-functional team (QA,
> micro, engineering, production) and version it like a living document. The
> single biggest Annex 1 finding is a CCS that exists but is never updated by
> what the monitoring data is actually telling you.
