# Quality Risk Management (ICH Q9)

ICH Q9(R1) provides a systematic process for assessing, controlling,
communicating, and reviewing risks to product quality across the lifecycle. The
guiding principle: risk effort should be **commensurate with the level of risk**.

## The QRM lifecycle

1. **Risk assessment** — identify hazards, analyze and evaluate risk.
2. **Risk control** — reduce or accept risk to an acceptable level.
3. **Risk communication** — share information among stakeholders.
4. **Risk review** — revisit outputs as new knowledge emerges.

## Common tools

- **FMEA** — Failure Mode and Effects Analysis; scores Severity × Occurrence ×
  Detection into a Risk Priority Number (RPN).
- **HACCP** — Hazard Analysis and Critical Control Points; identifies critical
  control points in a process.
- **Fault tree / PHA** — for top-down or preliminary hazard analysis.

## Avoiding subjectivity

The Q9(R1) revision specifically calls out **subjectivity** as a weakness.
Mitigate it with clearly defined scoring scales, cross-functional teams, and
explicit, documented rationale for each rating — not a single person's gut feel.

> Tip: a risk assessment is a living document. Tie it to change control so that
> changes trigger a re-review, rather than letting it go stale on a shared drive.
