# Change Control

Change control ensures that changes to GMP-relevant processes, equipment,
materials, methods, or documents are evaluated, approved, and implemented in a
controlled way — preventing unintended quality impact.

## The flow

1. **Raise** — describe the change and rationale.
2. **Assess impact** — quality, validation, regulatory, and risk implications; define required actions.
3. **Approve** — by Quality and relevant functions before implementation.
4. **Implement** — execute the agreed actions (revalidation, training, documents).
5. **Verify & close** — confirm the change achieved its objective without adverse effect.

## Planned vs unplanned

Change control is for **planned** changes. Unplanned departures are **deviations**
— don't use change control to retrofit something that already happened.

## Audit focus

Inspectors check that impact assessments are real (not tick-box), that all
linked actions are completed before closure, and that pending changes aren't
left open indefinitely.
