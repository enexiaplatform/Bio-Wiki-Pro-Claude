# GMP Training & Personnel Qualification

GMP requires that personnel are qualified by education, training, and experience
to do their jobs. An audit-proof training program proves people are not just
*informed* but *competent*.

## Role-based curricula

Training requirements should map to **job function**, not seniority. Define, per
role, the SOPs and GMP topics required, the method (read-and-understand,
classroom, on-the-job), and the assessment.

## "Read and understand" isn't competency

Signing that you read an SOP shows awareness, not ability. For tasks where errors
carry risk (aseptic technique, data entry, testing), use **assessed competency** —
a qualification with an observed or written check before the person works
unsupervised.

## Keep records audit-ready

Inspectors check: an up-to-date training matrix per person, completion records,
assessment results, and evidence that **retraining** happened when SOPs changed
or after related deviations. Gaps here are common findings.

## Retrain on change

Tie training to change control: a material SOP change should trigger retraining of
affected staff **before** they perform the revised task — not at the next annual
cycle.

> Tip: a training matrix that's green everywhere but never updated after SOP
> revisions is a red flag. Auditors cross-check training dates against SOP
> effective dates.
