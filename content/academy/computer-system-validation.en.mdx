# Computer System Validation (GAMP 5)

Computer System Validation (CSV) provides documented evidence that a GxP
computerized system does what it is supposed to do, consistently and reliably.
GAMP 5 (Second Edition) frames this as a **risk- and science-based** activity,
not a paperwork exercise.

## GAMP software categories

- **Category 1** — infrastructure software (operating systems, databases).
- **Category 3** — non-configured products used as supplied.
- **Category 4** — configured products (e.g. a configured LIMS).
- **Category 5** — custom / bespoke applications (highest risk, most rigor).

## The V-model

User requirements map to acceptance testing; functional specs map to functional
testing; design specs map to installation/operational qualification. Each left
arm has a corresponding right-arm verification.

## Data integrity drives scope

Validation effort should focus where data integrity risk is highest — audit
trails, access controls, electronic signatures, and ALCOA+ attributes. Leverage
supplier documentation for low-risk categories instead of duplicating it.

## Risk assessment — how to scope effort

For each function, score **impact** (patient safety / product quality / data
integrity) × **probability** of failure × **detectability**. High-risk functions
get scripted testing with evidence; low-risk functions can lean on supplier
testing and a documented rationale. Capture this in a simple worksheet:

| Function | GxP impact | Likelihood | Detectability | Risk | Test approach |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Result calculation | High | Low | Low | High | Scripted OQ + data check |
| Audit trail capture | High | Low | Medium | High | Verify on/off, content |
| Report cosmetic layout | Low | Low | High | Low | Leverage supplier docs |

## Periodic review checklist (post-go-live)

- Change history since last review (patches, config changes, all under change
  control?)
- User access list reviewed — leavers removed, privileges still appropriate?
- Audit trail enabled and being reviewed?
- Backups tested / restore verified?
- Open incidents, deviations, and CAPAs related to the system closed?
- Still validated for current intended use (no "creep" into new uses)?

## Cloud / SaaS GxP systems

The vendor may run the infrastructure, but **accountability stays with you**. Use
a **shared-responsibility matrix**, qualify the supplier (audit or assessment),
get their SOC 2 / validation summary, and pin down: change notification (you must
know before they push updates), data ownership and export on exit, and how their
audit trail meets ALCOA+. Validate the *configuration and intended use* — you
can't outsource the regulatory responsibility.

> Practitioner tip: GAMP 5 Second Edition explicitly discourages "testing
> everything." The defensible position is a documented risk rationale showing
> *why* you tested what you tested — and leveraged the supplier for the rest.
