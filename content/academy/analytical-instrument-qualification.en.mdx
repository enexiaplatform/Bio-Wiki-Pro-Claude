# Analytical Instrument Qualification (USP 1058)

USP 1058 defines Analytical Instrument Qualification (AIQ) — documented evidence
that an instrument is suitable for its intended use. AIQ is the base of the "data
quality triangle": without a qualified instrument, method validation and system
suitability mean little.

## The 4Qs

- **DQ — Design Qualification** — the instrument's design and the vendor are fit
  for purpose.
- **IQ — Installation Qualification** — correctly delivered and installed.
- **OQ — Operational Qualification** — performs to specification across its
  operating ranges.
- **PQ — Performance Qualification** — performs reliably for the actual method,
  in routine use.

## Risk-based instrument groups

- **Group A** — standard apparatus with no measurement capability (e.g. magnetic
  stirrers). Conformance to spec is usually enough.
- **Group B** — standard equipment providing measured values (e.g. balances, pH
  meters); IQ/OQ against specs.
- **Group C** — computerized analytical instruments (e.g. HPLC, GC-MS); full 4Qs
  plus software/data-integrity considerations.

## Why it matters

AIQ is where data integrity starts. An out-of-qualification instrument can
generate plausible-looking but invalid data — and every result that depends on
it becomes questionable.

> Tip: tie requalification and calibration to a schedule and to change control,
> and keep the qualification records as retrievable as your batch records.
