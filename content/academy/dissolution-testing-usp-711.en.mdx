# Dissolution Testing (USP 711)

Dissolution testing measures how much drug substance releases from a dosage form
into solution over time under standardized conditions. For oral solids it is a
critical quality attribute and a recognized surrogate for in-vivo bioavailability.

## The common apparatus

- **Apparatus 1 — Basket** — the dosage unit sits in a rotating basket; useful
  for capsules and units that float.
- **Apparatus 2 — Paddle** — a rotating paddle stirs the medium with the unit at
  the vessel bottom; the most widely used for tablets.

Other apparatus (reciprocating cylinder, flow-through cell) serve modified-release
and special dosage forms.

## The S1 / S2 / S3 acceptance stages

USP 711 uses a staged scheme: test 6 units (S1); if criteria aren't met, test 6
more (S2); and if still not met, 12 more (S3), with progressively defined
acceptance criteria. This controls the risk of passing or failing on too few
units.

## Why control parameters matter

Medium, volume, temperature (37°C), rotation speed, and de-aeration all affect
results. Small deviations change the dissolution profile — which is why method
parameters are tightly specified and qualified.

> Tip: profile comparisons (e.g. the f2 similarity factor) are how you judge
> whether a formulation or process change altered release behavior.
