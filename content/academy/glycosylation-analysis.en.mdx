# Glycosylation Analysis

Most therapeutic antibodies and many recombinant proteins are **glycosylated** —
sugar chains attached during cell-culture. Glycans aren't cosmetic: they shape
function, so the **glycan profile is a critical quality attribute** and one of the
most process-sensitive things QC monitors.

## Why glycans matter

- **Effector function** — Fc glycans tune **ADCC**. Notably, **afucosylation
  raises ADCC** (more potent killing) — exploited deliberately in some mAbs.
- **Half-life / clearance** — exposed galactose and especially **high-mannose**
  or sialylation patterns affect serum half-life via receptor clearance.
- **Immunogenicity** — non-human glycans (e.g. α-Gal, NGNA) can be immunogenic.

Because cell-culture conditions (media, pH, feeds, harvest timing) shift glycans,
the profile is a sensitive readout of process consistency.

## How glycans are measured

- **Released-glycan profiling** — enzymatically release N-glycans (PNGase F),
  label (e.g. 2-AB or RapiFluor), and separate by **HILIC** with fluorescence
  ± MS. Gives the relative % of each glycan species (G0F, G1F, G2F, Man5, etc.).
- **Site-specific / glycopeptide mapping** — LC-MS on glycopeptides shows which
  site carries which glycan.
- **Monosaccharide / sialic acid assays** — composition and sialylation content.
- **Released MS (e.g. MALDI)** — confirms structures.

## What QC controls

Release and characterization typically monitor key species and groups: total
afucosylation (ties to potency/ADCC), high-mannose (clearance), galactosylation,
and sialylation — each with ranges justified by their functional impact.

## Investigating a glycan shift

A drift in, say, high-mannose almost always points **upstream**: a media lot,
feed strategy, temperature, or harvest-day change in cell culture. Glycan trends
are a leading indicator of process change — often before other CQAs move.

> Practitioner tip: link each monitored glycan to *why* it's controlled
> (afucosylation→ADCC potency; high-mannose→clearance; NGNA/α-Gal→immunogenicity).
> A glycan spec without that functional rationale is hard to defend — and harder
> to investigate when it drifts.
