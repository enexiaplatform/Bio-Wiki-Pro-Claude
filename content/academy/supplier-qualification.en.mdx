# Supplier Qualification

Supplier qualification is the documented process for deciding whether a supplier
can provide a material, component, or service without creating unacceptable
quality risk. ICH Q10 treats outsourced activities and purchased materials as
part of the pharmaceutical quality system, so accountability remains with the
regulated company.

## Risk-based tiers

Qualification depth should follow risk:

- **High risk** - API, primary packaging, sterile component, contract
  sterilization, critical testing, sole-source material. Usually requires deep
  technical review, audit, quality agreement, and close monitoring.
- **Medium risk** - important excipient, secondary packaging, routine outsourced
  service, or supplier with mixed performance. Usually requires documentation
  review, possible remote audit, quality agreement where justified, and periodic
  review.
- **Low risk** - indirect or noncritical materials with limited product impact.
  Questionnaire and documentation review may be enough.

## Define the approved scope

"Approved supplier" is incomplete unless the scope is clear. The file should say:

- material, grade, specification, and intended use;
- manufacturing site and testing site;
- distributor or broker role, if any;
- approved route of supply;
- whether supplier CoA results can be used and for which tests;
- special restrictions or extra incoming checks.

## Key elements

- Initial risk assessment.
- Supplier questionnaire, certification, technical review, and audit where
  justified.
- Approved supplier list with exact scope.
- Quality agreement for critical suppliers and outsourced GMP activities.
- Incoming verification and CoA review.
- Ongoing monitoring of defects, deviations, complaints, CoA issues, and audit
  findings.
- Risk-based requalification or for-cause review.

## Quality agreement checklist

- [ ] Scope: exact materials, services, and sites covered.
- [ ] Specifications and CoA content agreed.
- [ ] Responsibilities for testing, release, storage, and rejected material.
- [ ] Change notification before changes to material, process, site, method, or
      sub-tier supplier.
- [ ] Deviation, OOS, complaint, recall, and inspection communication timelines.
- [ ] Right to audit and audit cadence.
- [ ] Record retention, traceability, and recall cooperation.
- [ ] Sub-tier supplier control.

## Ongoing monitoring

Qualification is a lifecycle. Trend incoming results, CoA discrepancies,
supplier-related deviations, complaints, audit findings, and late change
notifications. A poor trend should trigger reassessment even if the calendar
requalification date has not arrived.
