# Supplier Qualification

Your product is only as reliable as your supply chain. Supplier qualification
provides documented assurance that materials and services meet quality
requirements — a core ICH Q10 expectation.

## Risk-based tiers

- **Critical** (e.g. APIs, primary packaging, sterilization services) → on-site
  audit + quality agreement.
- **Non-critical** → questionnaire / documentation review may suffice.

## Key elements

- Initial assessment (questionnaire, samples, audit)
- **Quality agreement** defining responsibilities
- Approved Supplier List with scope
- **Ongoing monitoring** — incoming results, complaints, requalification cadence

## The audit trap

A one-time approval is not qualification. Auditors look for *ongoing* performance
review and a defined requalification interval driven by risk.

## Supplier-risk scoring matrix

Score each supplier on the factors below; the total drives the qualification
depth and requalification interval.

| Factor | Low (1) | Medium (2) | High (3) |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Material criticality | Indirect/non-contact | Excipient/secondary pack | API, primary pack, sterilization |
| Process impact | Cosmetic | Affects attribute | Affects sterility/safety |
| Supplier history | Long, no issues | Some findings | New / prior quality issues |
| Substitutability | Many sources | Few | Sole source |
| Regulatory status | Routinely inspected | Mixed | Unregistered / unknown |

- **Total 5–7 (Low):** questionnaire + CoA review; requalify ~3 yearly.
- **Total 8–11 (Medium):** documentation + possible remote audit; ~2 yearly.
- **Total 12–15 (High):** on-site audit + quality agreement; annual review.

## Quality agreement checklist

- [ ] Scope: exact materials/services and sites covered.
- [ ] Specifications and CoA content/format agreed.
- [ ] **Change notification** — supplier must notify *before* any change to
      material, process, or site (the most-cited gap).
- [ ] Deviation, OOS, and complaint communication and timelines.
- [ ] Right to audit and audit cadence.
- [ ] Sub-tier supplier control (does your supplier qualify *their* suppliers?).
- [ ] Record retention, traceability, and recall cooperation.
- [ ] Responsibilities for testing, release, and rejected-material handling.

## Ongoing monitoring

Qualification is a lifecycle: trend incoming inspection results, CoA-vs-test
discrepancies, complaints, and on-time/in-full delivery. A rising defect trend
should trigger re-assessment regardless of the calendar interval.

> Practitioner tip: the change-notification clause is where supply-chain
> surprises are prevented. The most expensive deviations start with a supplier
> "improving" a process they never told you about.
