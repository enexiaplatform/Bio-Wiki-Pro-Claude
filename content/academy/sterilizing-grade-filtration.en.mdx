# Sterilizing-Grade Filtration & Integrity Testing

When a product can't survive terminal sterilization, sterilizing-grade filtration
(typically a 0.22 µm membrane) removes microorganisms during aseptic processing.
EU GMP Annex 1 sets clear expectations for redundancy and integrity testing.

## Filter integrity tests

A sterilizing filter must be proven intact before and after use:

- **Bubble point** — the pressure at which gas pushes liquid out of the largest
  pore; correlates to the maximum pore size and microbial retention.
- **Diffusive flow / forward flow** — measures gas diffusion through the wetted
  membrane below the bubble point; preferred for large-area filters.
- **Pressure hold** — a decay-based variant of diffusive flow.

The test limits are tied to the manufacturer's validated bacterial-retention data
(the *Brevundimonas diminuta* challenge).

## Pre- and post-use testing

Annex 1 expects integrity testing **after use** (mandatory) and, where practical,
**before use after sterilization (PUPSIT)** — a point of ongoing industry debate
that your Contamination Control Strategy must address with a documented rationale.

## PUPSIT — a decision framework

PUPSIT (pre-use, post-sterilization integrity testing) guards against a filter
flaw that is masked during use ("flaw-masking" by product components or process).
Annex 1 expects you to either do PUPSIT or document a robust risk rationale for
not doing it. Weigh:

- **For PUPSIT:** detects a damaged/incorrectly installed filter before product is
  exposed; strongest where flaw-masking is plausible.
- **Against PUPSIT:** the test setup itself adds aseptic connections and
  complexity (its own contamination risk), and can wet/condition the filter.

Document the assessment per product/process in the CCS — there is no universal
right answer, only a justified one.

## Wetting and test-gas pitfalls (false failures)

Most "integrity failures" are method errors, not bad filters:

- **Incomplete wetting** — a dry spot reads as a leak; flush sufficient volume and
  respect soak time.
- **Wrong wetting fluid** — test parameters are fluid-specific (water vs product).
- **Temperature drift / gas not equilibrated** — diffusion is temperature
  sensitive; let the system stabilize.
- **Upstream volume and connections** — leaks at fittings mimic membrane failure.

Confirm a suspected failure by re-wetting and retesting before declaring the
filter (and the batch) compromised.

## Redundant filtration

Annex 1 favors a **second sterilizing filter** close to the point of fill as risk
mitigation, especially when post-use integrity can only be tested at the end of a
long run. If the primary fails post-use, the redundant filter supports an impact
assessment — but redundancy is a safety net, not a license to skip integrity
testing or PUPSIT reasoning.

> Practitioner tip: capture the filter lot, wetting fluid, test parameters, and
> upstream setup with every integrity test. When a result is borderline, that
> record is what separates a true failure from a fixable method artifact.
