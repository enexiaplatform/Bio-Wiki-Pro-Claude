# Sterilizing-Grade Filtration & Integrity Testing

When a product can't survive terminal sterilization, sterilizing-grade filtration
(typically a 0.22 µm membrane) removes microorganisms during aseptic processing.
EU GMP Annex 1 sets clear expectations for redundancy and integrity testing.

## Filter integrity tests

A sterilizing filter must be proven intact before and after use:

- **Bubble point** — the pressure at which gas pushes liquid out of the largest
  pore; correlates to the maximum pore size and microbial retention.
- **Diffusive flow / forward flow** — measures gas diffusion through the wetted
  membrane below the bubble point; preferred for large-area filters.
- **Pressure hold** — a decay-based variant of diffusive flow.

The test limits are tied to the manufacturer's validated bacterial-retention data
(the *Brevundimonas diminuta* challenge).

## Pre- and post-use testing

Annex 1 expects integrity testing **after use** (mandatory) and, where practical,
**before use after sterilization (PUPSIT)** — a point of ongoing industry debate
that your Contamination Control Strategy must address with a documented rationale.
