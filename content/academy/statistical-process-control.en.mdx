# Statistical Process Control & Trending

Statistical Process Control (SPC) turns raw quality data into a signal you can
act on. Under the lifecycle approach to validation, **Stage 3 — Continued Process
Verification** depends on it.

## Common vs special cause

- **Common cause** — the inherent, random variation of a stable process. You do
  not investigate individual points; you improve the system.
- **Special cause** — an assignable, unusual signal. This is what triggers
  investigation.

Confusing the two leads to either over-reacting to noise or missing real shifts.

## Reading a control chart

Control limits are derived from the process data itself (typically ±3 sigma) —
**not** from the specification. A process can be in statistical control yet still
incapable of meeting spec, which is why capability (Cpk) is assessed separately.

The Western Electric rules flag special causes: a point beyond a control limit,
runs on one side of the mean, trends, and other non-random patterns.

## Which chart for which data

- **I-MR (individuals & moving range)** — one value per batch (most common in
  pharma CPV: assay, content, fill weight averages).
- **Xbar-R / Xbar-S** — subgroups (e.g. multiple tablets per batch).
- **p / np charts** — proportion defective (attribute data, e.g. % rejects).
- **EWMA / CUSUM** — sensitive to small, sustained drifts; good early warning.

Match the chart to the data structure; an I-MR chart on data that really has
subgroups hides within-subgroup variation.

## Alert vs action vs control limits

| Limit | Source | Meaning | Response |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Control (±3σ) | Process data | Statistical boundary of common cause | Investigate special cause |
| Alert | Often ±2σ or internal | Early warning | Heightened attention, no rejection |
| Action | Internal, risk-based | Pre-spec intervention threshold | Defined action / investigation |
| Specification | Registered/compendial | Pass/fail for the product | OOS handling |

Crucially, **control ≠ spec**. A point can breach a control limit (a real process
signal) while still passing spec — that's exactly the early signal SPC exists to
catch.

## Western Electric rules (special-cause signals)

1. One point beyond 3σ.
2. Two of three consecutive points beyond 2σ (same side).
3. Four of five beyond 1σ (same side).
4. Eight consecutive points on one side of the mean (a shift).

Plus runs and trends (e.g. seven steadily rising points). Each pattern is too
unlikely to be random — it flags an assignable cause.

## Wiring SPC into CPV and the APR

Stage 3 Continued Process Verification *is* ongoing SPC: chart CQAs/CPPs batch to
batch, react to special causes through deviation/CAPA, and reduce common-cause
variation through improvement. The **Annual Product Review** aggregates these
charts to confirm the process stayed in control and capable across the year, and
to re-justify limits.

> Practitioner tip: don't investigate every wiggle. Reacting to common-cause noise
> ("tampering") actually *increases* variation. Investigate signals defined by the
> rules — and leave a stable process alone while you work on the system.
