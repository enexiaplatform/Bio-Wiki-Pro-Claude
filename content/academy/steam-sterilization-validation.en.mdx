# Steam Sterilization Validation (Autoclaves)

Moist-heat (steam) sterilization is the workhorse for heat-stable items.
Validation proves the cycle reliably achieves the required Sterility Assurance
Level (SAL of 10^-6) for the defined load.

## F0 - lethality in one number

**F0** is the equivalent exposure time in minutes at 121 degrees C delivering the
same lethality as the actual cycle. It lets you compare and combine cycles run
at different temperatures against a single lethality target.

## Overkill vs bioburden/biological-indicator approaches

- **Overkill** - demonstrate a large spore-log reduction (commonly >=12-log of a
  resistant BI, or >=6-log with safety margin) without needing detailed product
  bioburden knowledge. Simplest and most common for robust items.
- **Bioburden / BI-bioburden** - used when the product cannot withstand
  overkill; relies on characterized bioburden resistance.

## What gets qualified

- **Temperature distribution** (empty + loaded chamber) to find the cold spot.
- **Heat penetration** to the hardest-to-sterilize location in the load.
- **Biological indicators** (*Geobacillus stearothermophilus* spores) at the
  cold spots confirming the spore-log reduction.
- **Defined load patterns** - you can only claim what you qualified; new loads
  need qualification.

> Tip: the cold spot is the whole game. Map it empty and loaded, place BIs there,
> and never run a load configuration you did not validate.
