# Good Documentation Practice (GDocP)

Good Documentation Practice is the foundation of data integrity. If it isn't
documented correctly, regulators treat it as if it didn't happen. GDocP turns
the ALCOA+ principles into day-to-day behavior.

## ALCOA+ at a glance

- **Attributable** — who recorded it, and when.
- **Legible** — readable and permanent.
- **Contemporaneous** — recorded at the time of the activity.
- **Original** — the first capture (or a verified true copy).
- **Accurate** — correct, with no editing that hides the original.
- *Plus:* Complete, Consistent, Enduring, Available.

## Correcting an error the right way

Draw a **single line** through the entry so the original remains readable, then
initial, date, and add a brief reason. Never overwrite, erase, or use correction
fluid — those are classic data-integrity findings.

## Habits that keep you audit-ready

- Record in real time — not from memory at the end of the shift.
- No blank fields: use N/A with initials where an entry doesn't apply.
- Sign for your own work only; pre-signing or back-dating is falsification.

> Tip: the cheapest data-integrity control is culture. Teams that record
> contemporaneously and correct errors openly rarely generate serious findings.
