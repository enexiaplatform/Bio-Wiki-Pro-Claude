# Analytical Method Transfer

Method transfer is the documented process that qualifies a receiving laboratory
to run a validated method with the same reliability as the originating lab. A
failed transfer stalls product release, so the protocol design matters.

## Transfer approaches

- **Comparative testing** — both labs test the same samples; results are
  compared against predefined acceptance criteria.
- **Co-validation** — the receiving lab participates in the original validation.
- **Revalidation** — the receiving lab repeats validation in full.
- **Transfer waiver** — justified when the method is compendial or the receiving
  lab already has documented equivalent experience.

## Designing the protocol

Define acceptance criteria for accuracy and precision *before* testing, use
representative (ideally aged or stressed) samples, and confirm both labs share
equivalent instruments, reference standards, and analyst training.

## Setting comparative-testing acceptance criteria

Two common, defensible approaches for an assay transfer:

- **Difference in means** — |mean(RU) − mean(SU)| ≤ a pre-set limit (e.g. ≤2% for
  a drug assay), justified against the method's precision and the product spec.
- **Inter-lab precision** — combined %RSD across both labs within a limit (e.g.
  ≤3%), often with 3 analysts × 3 batches/lots × replicate preparations.

For impurities, set absolute limits at low levels (e.g. ±0.05% or ±25% relative,
whichever is larger) because percentage limits explode near the LOQ. Always tie
the number to *what the result is used to decide*, not to whatever looks easy to
pass.

## Transfer-readiness checklist

- [ ] Method validated at the SU; report and validated range available to RU.
- [ ] Reference standards, columns, reagents, and critical consumables sourced.
- [ ] RU instruments equivalent and qualified (AIQ); same detector type/settings.
- [ ] RU analysts trained on the method; a dry run completed.
- [ ] Representative samples (ideally aged/stressed to show impurities) prepared.
- [ ] Protocol with approach, samples, replicates, and acceptance criteria approved
      by both QA units before any reportable testing.

## Worked transfer-failure investigation

*Event:* RU assay biased ~3% low vs SU, failing the difference-in-means criterion.
**Investigation:** standards and samples ruled out; column lot matched; the RU's
sample prep used a different sonication time, leaving the analyte incompletely
extracted. **Root cause:** an under-specified extraction step in the method (a
robustness gap, not an analyst error). **Action:** tighten the method's
extraction instructions, re-run the transfer; feed the robustness gap back to the
SU's method owner (ICH Q14 lifecycle).

> Practitioner tip: most transfer failures trace to under-specified sample prep,
> not the instrument. The fix usually belongs in the *method* — which is why a
> transfer often surfaces robustness gaps the original validation missed.
