# OOS Investigation — Deep Dive

An out-of-specification (OOS) investigation has two phases per the **FDA OOS
Guidance (2006)**.

## Phase I — Laboratory assessment

Identify a clear, assignable laboratory error (technique, equipment failure,
calculation mistake) before widening the investigation.

## Phase II — Full-scale investigation

When no lab cause is found, expand into manufacturing: materials, process,
batch history.
