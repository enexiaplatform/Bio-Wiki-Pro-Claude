# Visual Inspection of Injectables

Every parenteral unit undergoes 100% visual inspection for visible particulate
matter and container/closure defects, per **USP &lt;790&gt;** and the guidance in
**USP &lt;1790&gt;**.

## The two-step model

1. **100% inspection** — every unit is inspected (manual, semi-, or automated).
2. **AQL inspection** — a statistical sample of the accepted batch confirms the
   process, against defect category limits (critical/major/minor).

## Defect classification

- **Critical:** could cause harm (e.g. large particulate, cracked container).
- **Major / minor:** cosmetic or lower-risk defects with looser AQLs.

## Operator qualification

Manual inspection depends on the inspector — qualification includes vision
testing and challenge sets, with periodic re-qualification. Fatigue management
(inspection time limits) is expected.
