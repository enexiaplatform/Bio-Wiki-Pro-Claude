# Container Closure Integrity Testing (CCIT)

Container Closure Integrity Testing demonstrates that a package maintains a
sterile barrier and prevents product loss throughout shelf life. USP 1207 shifts
the field toward **deterministic**, instrument-based methods over probabilistic
probe tests.

## Deterministic vs probabilistic

- **Deterministic** — physicochemical methods with quantitative, repeatable
  signals (vacuum decay, high-voltage leak detection, headspace analysis).
- **Probabilistic** — methods whose result depends on chance (dye ingress,
  microbial immersion); higher variability and harder to validate.

## The maximum allowable leakage limit (MALL)

CCIT methods are validated against the MALL — the leak size below which neither
sterility nor product quality is compromised. Method sensitivity must reliably
detect leaks at or near that limit.
