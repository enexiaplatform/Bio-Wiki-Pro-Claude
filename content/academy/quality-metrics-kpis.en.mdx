# Quality Metrics & KPIs

Quality metrics turn a quality system's health into numbers leadership and
regulators can track. FDA has signaled interest in quality-metrics programs such
as lot acceptance, OOS, and complaints, and a mature site trends these
proactively.

## Metrics that matter

- **Lot Acceptance Rate (LAR)**: share of lots accepted vs. rejected; a basic
  output measure.
- **Right-First-Time (RFT)**: batches produced with no deviations/errors; a strong
  process-health signal.
- **Invalidated OOS rate**: OOS results attributed to lab error. A high rate is a
  red flag for testing-into-compliance or weak investigations.
- **On-time CAPA / deviation closure**: are issues resolved within target?
- **Complaint and recall rates**: the voice of the patient/customer.

## The gaming trap

The moment a metric drives a bonus or a target in isolation, behavior bends to the
metric: deviations go unrecorded, OOS get reclassified, closures get rushed. Use
a **balanced set** of metrics and watch for suspicious improvements, such as
deviations dropping while complaints rise.

## Make them actionable

A metric you don't act on is decoration. Each KPI needs an owner, a target, a
trend review cadence, and an escalation path when it drifts.

> Tip: pair every output metric (LAR) with a behavior metric (RFT, deviation
> reporting rate) so improvements reflect real quality, not better hiding.
