# Viral Safety of Biologics

Biologics made in mammalian cells could, in principle, carry viruses — from the
cell line itself or introduced during manufacture. Because no single test can
prove a product is virus-free, **ICH Q5A** builds safety on **three complementary
pillars**. No one pillar is sufficient alone.

## Pillar 1 — Control the source

Start clean: characterize and qualify the **cell banks** (master and working) for
endogenous and adventitious viruses, and control raw materials of biological
origin (media components, etc.). A well-characterized source minimizes what could
be present.

## Pillar 2 — Test at appropriate stages

Test the cell banks, unprocessed bulk, and (as relevant) in-process material for
viruses using methods such as:

- **In vitro / in vivo adventitious agent assays**
- **Retrovirus testing** (TEM, infectivity, RT assays for endogenous retrovirus)
- **Specific PCR / NGS** for known and emerging agents

Testing detects what's there — but its limit is that absence of a signal is not
proof of absence of every possible agent.

## Pillar 3 — Validated viral clearance

The decisive pillar: demonstrate the **purification process removes or inactivates
virus**. In scaled-down studies, deliberately **spike** model viruses before
specific steps and measure the **log reduction value (LRV)**:

- **Removal** — chromatography, and especially **virus filtration** (nanofiltration).
- **Inactivation** — low-pH hold, solvent/detergent, heat.

Total clearance (sum of orthogonal steps) must give a large **safety margin** over
any conceivable viral load.

## Why all three

Source control reduces risk, testing catches contamination, and clearance provides
the quantitative margin that covers the unknown. Regulators evaluate the
**combination** — a strong clearance margin is what lets you ship despite the
theoretical limits of testing.

## Why it matters

Viral safety is non-negotiable for mammalian-cell biologics and a specialized,
respected QC/validation area. Understanding the three-pillar logic — especially
spiking studies and LRV — is core biologics literacy.

> Tip: when asked "how do you know it's virus-safe?", the strong answer is the
> three pillars together, with the emphasis on validated clearance margin — not on
> any single negative test result.
