# The Pharmaceutical Quality System (ICH Q10)

ICH Q10 describes a **Pharmaceutical Quality System (PQS)**: the framework that
holds all the GMP pieces together across the product lifecycle, from development
to discontinuation. It builds on ISO concepts and GMP, and complements ICH Q8
(design) and Q9 (risk).

## Three objectives

1. **Achieve product realization**: deliver products with the right quality
   attributes.
2. **Establish and maintain a state of control**: process and quality systems
   performing as intended.
3. **Facilitate continual improvement.**

## Four PQS elements

- **Process performance & product quality monitoring system.**
- **CAPA system.**
- **Change management system.**
- **Management review** of process performance and quality.

Two **enablers** run through all of it: **knowledge management** and **quality
risk management (Q9)**.

## Management responsibility

Q10 is explicit that **senior management** owns the quality system by providing
resources, setting quality policy/objectives, and conducting management reviews.
Quality is not only "the QA department's problem"; leadership is accountable.

## Across the lifecycle

The same PQS spans **development -> technology transfer -> commercial
manufacturing -> product discontinuation**, so knowledge and control carry
forward rather than restarting at each stage.

> Tip: when an auditor asks "show me your quality system," they mean these
> elements working together: monitoring feeding CAPA and change control, reviewed
> by management, not a binder of SOPs.
