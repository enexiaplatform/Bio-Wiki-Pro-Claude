# Gowning & Personnel Qualification

People shed microorganisms constantly, so cleanroom gowning — and proving each
operator gowns correctly — is a frontline contamination control under Annex 1.

## Initial qualification

A new operator must demonstrate correct, contamination-free gowning, typically
across **three consecutive successful sessions** assessed with microbial contact
plates at key sites (gloves, forearms, chest, hood, face mask area).

## Periodic re-qualification

Re-qualify at a defined frequency (risk-based, e.g. annually for Grade A/B) and
after any failed personnel monitoring or extended absence.

## Disqualification triggers

- Repeated personnel-monitoring excursions
- Failed media fill linked to technique
- Observed poor gowning practice

## Why it matters

Gowning qualification ties directly to media-fill success and EM trends — a weak
gowning program shows up as recurring Grade A/B excursions.
