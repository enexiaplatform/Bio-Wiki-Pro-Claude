# Electronic Records & Signatures (21 CFR Part 11 / Annex 11)

When records and signatures move from paper to computer systems, two regulations
define what "trustworthy" means: **FDA 21 CFR Part 11** (US) and **EU GMP Annex
11** (EU). They are the technical backbone of data integrity.

## What Part 11 requires of electronic records

- **Validation** of the system for its intended use.
- **Audit trails** — secure, computer-generated, time-stamped, recording who
  did what and when, without overwriting prior entries.
- **Access controls** — only authorized individuals; unique accounts (no shared
  logins).
- **Copies** — the ability to generate accurate, complete copies for inspection.
- **Record retention** for the required period, retrievable throughout.

## What makes an electronic signature compliant

- Uniquely attributable to one individual; never reused or reassigned.
- Linked to its record so it can't be copied or transferred.
- Shows the **signed meaning** (e.g. review, approval) plus the signer and
  timestamp.
- Two distinct components on first signing (e.g. user ID + password).

## Annex 11 — the EU counterpart

Annex 11 covers similar ground (risk management, validation, data, audit trails,
e-signatures) and is read together with EU GMP Chapter 4. A key theme: **risk-
based** controls proportionate to the system's GMP impact.

## Access-control matrix

Map roles to least-privilege permissions and review it periodically:

| Role | Acquire data | Reprocess | Modify method | Delete | Admin/config | Approve |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Analyst | ✔ | ✔ (logged) | ✘ | ✘ | ✘ | ✘ |
| Reviewer / QA | view | ✘ | ✘ | ✘ | ✘ | ✔ |
| Lab supervisor | ✔ | ✔ (logged) | ✔ (controlled) | ✘ | ✘ | ✘ |
| System admin (IT) | ✘ | ✘ | ✘ | ✘ | ✔ | ✘ |

The cardinal rule: **no one who generates data should be able to delete it or
disable its audit trail.** Admin rights belong to people without a stake in the
result. Separate the "do the work" and "configure the system" duties.

## Part 11 / Annex 11 gap-assessment checklist

- [ ] System validated for intended use; validation current after upgrades.
- [ ] Audit trail on, secure, time-stamped, and **reviewed** (with records).
- [ ] Unique user accounts; no shared logins; password/lockout policy enforced.
- [ ] Role-based access follows least privilege; periodic access review done.
- [ ] E-signatures meet the two-component + signed-meaning + linkage rules.
- [ ] Accurate, complete copies producible for inspection (human-readable export).
- [ ] Time source synchronized and controlled (no local clock edits).
- [ ] Backup/restore validated; retention and retrievability assured.
- [ ] Open/closed system controls appropriate (encryption for open systems).

## Open vs closed systems

A **closed** system limits access to people responsible for the content (most
on-prem GxP systems). An **open** system (data crosses parties who don't control
access) adds requirements like encryption and additional signature controls —
relevant for some cloud/exchange scenarios.

> Practitioner tip: Part 11 is not about having the most features — it's about
> attributability and an audit trail you actually review. A simple system with
> unique logins and a reviewed trail beats a feature-rich one run on a shared
> "admin" account.
