# Electronic Records & Signatures (21 CFR Part 11 / Annex 11)

When records and signatures move from paper to computer systems, two regulations
define what "trustworthy" means: **FDA 21 CFR Part 11** (US) and **EU GMP Annex
11** (EU). They are the technical backbone of data integrity.

## What Part 11 requires of electronic records

- **Validation** of the system for its intended use.
- **Audit trails** — secure, computer-generated, time-stamped, recording who
  did what and when, without overwriting prior entries.
- **Access controls** — only authorized individuals; unique accounts (no shared
  logins).
- **Copies** — the ability to generate accurate, complete copies for inspection.
- **Record retention** for the required period, retrievable throughout.

## What makes an electronic signature compliant

- Uniquely attributable to one individual; never reused or reassigned.
- Linked to its record so it can't be copied or transferred.
- Shows the **signed meaning** (e.g. review, approval) plus the signer and
  timestamp.
- Two distinct components on first signing (e.g. user ID + password).

## Annex 11 — the EU counterpart

Annex 11 covers similar ground (risk management, validation, data, audit trails,
e-signatures) and is read together with EU GMP Chapter 4. A key theme: **risk-
based** controls proportionate to the system's GMP impact.
