# Recall management: the real-world test of your traceability

A recall is the one quality process you hope never to use in earnest — and the one
that punishes any weakness mercilessly. When a defect serious enough to remove product
from the market is confirmed, everything you have built around **traceability,
distribution records, and decision-making** gets tested at once, under time pressure
and scrutiny.

## What a recall actually requires

To pull a batch back from the field, you need to answer fast:

- **What is affected?** Precise batch traceability — which lots, which units.
- **Where did it go?** Distribution records good enough to reach every customer who
  received it.
- **How serious is it?** A health-hazard assessment that drives the recall **class**
  (from a defect likely to cause serious harm, down to a minor labelling issue).
- **Who decides and notifies?** A clear chain — internal decision, customer
  notification, and reporting to the relevant authorities within required timelines.

## Speed and reach are the whole game

The two metrics that matter are how **fast** you can act and how completely you can
**reach** the affected product. A recall that is slow or misses distribution points
leaves defective product in use — which is exactly the harm the recall exists to
prevent. That is why distribution and traceability records are treated as critical,
not clerical.

## The mock recall is the part that saves you

You cannot wait for a real recall to discover your records have gaps. A **mock recall**
— periodically tracing a batch from manufacture to its endpoints within a target time —
is the rehearsal that proves the system works. The teams that recall well are the ones
that practised; the ones that struggle usually never tested their traceability until it
was real.

## Where it connects

Recall management sits downstream of complaints and investigations — they are often
what reveal a recallable defect — and it draws on the same traceability that batch
release depends on. A strong upstream quality system is the best way to make recalls
rare and survivable.
