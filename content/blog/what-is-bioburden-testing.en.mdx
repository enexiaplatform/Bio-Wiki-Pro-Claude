# What Is Bioburden Testing?

Bioburden is the population of viable microorganisms on or in a product before
it's sterilized. **Bioburden testing** counts them — a foundational microbiology
QC test for sterile and non-sterile products alike.

## Why it matters

You can't validate a sterilization process without knowing the starting microbial
load. Bioburden data feeds sterilization validation (the dose or cycle needed to
achieve sterility), and trending it flags contamination problems early.

## What gets measured

- **Total Aerobic Microbial Count (TAMC)** — bacteria.
- **Total Yeast and Mould Count (TYMC)** — fungi.
- Sometimes **specified organisms** depending on the product and route.

Samples are typically enumerated by membrane filtration or plate methods on
suitable media, then incubated at defined temperatures.

## The standards

- **USP &lt;61&gt;/&lt;62&gt;** and **EP 2.6.12/2.6.13** for non-sterile product microbial
  examination.
- **ISO 11737-1** for bioburden on medical devices ahead of sterilization.

## The detail people miss: recovery efficiency

Your method doesn't recover 100% of organisms from the product. Method validation
establishes a **recovery efficiency**, and the raw count is corrected for it.
Skipping this under-reports the true bioburden.
