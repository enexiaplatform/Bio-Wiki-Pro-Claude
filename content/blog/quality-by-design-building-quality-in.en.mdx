# Quality by Design: building quality in, not testing it in

The old way to develop a product was largely empirical: make it, test it, and fix
what fails. Quality by Design (QbD), framed in **ICH Q8**, is the modern
alternative — design the product and process from an **understanding** of what drives
quality, so quality is built in rather than inspected in afterwards. It is the
conceptual backbone that ties together specifications, validation, and control.

## The QbD vocabulary, connected

QbD has a handful of terms that only make sense as a chain:

- **QTPP (Quality Target Product Profile)** — what the product needs to be and do for
  the patient. Everything starts here.
- **Critical Quality Attributes (CQAs)** — the properties that must be controlled to
  meet the QTPP (e.g. purity, potency, dissolution).
- **Critical Process Parameters (CPPs)** — the process settings that affect the CQAs.
- **Design space** — the multidimensional range of CPPs (and material attributes)
  proven to deliver acceptable CQAs. Working inside it is not considered a change.
- **Control strategy** — the planned set of controls (in-process, specifications,
  monitoring) that keeps the process delivering the CQAs.

Read top to bottom, it is a logic: patient need → quality attributes → the
parameters that drive them → the proven operating space → how you control it.

## Why it changes QC's job

Under QbD, the specification is not an arbitrary line — it is **derived** from what
the patient needs and what the process can do. Testing shifts from a gate that
catches failures to a confirmation that a well-understood process is behaving. It is
also what makes **continued process verification** and risk-based control rational
rather than bureaucratic.

## The honest caveat

QbD is a direction, not a box to tick. A real design space comes from real process
understanding and experimentation; a "design space" asserted without data is just a
wide specification wearing a fancier name.
