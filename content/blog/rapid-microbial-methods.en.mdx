# Rapid Microbial Methods: are they audit-ready?

Rapid microbial methods (RMM) — ATP bioluminescence, flow cytometry, growth-based
detection — can cut days off product release. But adopting them is a validation
project, not a plug-and-play swap.

## What auditors scrutinise

- **Equivalence** to the compendial method (USP &lt;1223&gt; / Ph. Eur. 5.1.6):
  demonstrate the RMM is at least as good for your matrix.
- **Validation parameters:** accuracy, precision, specificity, limit of
  detection, robustness, ruggedness.
- **Method suitability** for each product, not just a generic claim.

## Practical advice

Start RMM where the ROI is clearest (e.g. water monitoring or in-process
bioburden) and keep a documented equivalence package. The technology rarely
fails an audit — the **validation documentation** is what does.
