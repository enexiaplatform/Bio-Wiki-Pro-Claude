# USP, EP, JP: Pharmacopoeias Explained

A **pharmacopoeia** is a legally recognized collection of quality standards and
test methods for medicines. Three dominate global pharma QC:

- **USP** — United States Pharmacopeia (US, plus widely used elsewhere).
- **EP (Ph. Eur.)** — European Pharmacopoeia.
- **JP** — Japanese Pharmacopoeia.

## Why there are several

Each region's authority maintains its own compendium with the force of law in
that market. A product sold in the US must meet USP; in the EU, EP. For global
products, QC labs often need to satisfy more than one.

## Harmonization — they're converging

Two efforts reduce the duplication:

- **PDG** (Pharmacopoeial Discussion Group) harmonizes common general chapters and
  excipient monographs across USP, EP, and JP.
- **ICH Q4B** evaluates harmonized chapters and declares them **interchangeable**
  across ICH regions (e.g. sterility, endotoxin, microbial enumeration).

Where a method is Q4B-harmonized, passing one usually satisfies the others — but
you must cite the right chapter for the target market.

## How a QC lab chooses

Follow the **registered method** for the product's market. Where harmonized,
document which pharmacopoeia/edition you used. Watch for **non-harmonized
differences** (acceptance criteria, reagent grades) that can change a pass/fail.
