# How to Prepare for a GMP Audit in 30 Days

A GMP inspection notice doesn't have to trigger panic. With a focused four-week
plan, you can walk in ready. Here's a realistic schedule.

## Week 1 — Take stock

- Pull your **open items list**: deviations, OOS/OOT, CAPAs, change controls.
  Anything overdue is a red flag — prioritise closure or document a sound
  justification.
- Confirm key documents are **retrievable in minutes**: batch records, SOPs,
  training matrices, calibration and qualification records.

## Week 2 — Fix the obvious gaps

- Review **audit trails** for the systems that determine release. Make sure
  review is documented, not reconstructed on demand.
- Check **training records** against recent SOP revisions — a classic mismatch.
- Verify reference standards, calibrations, and qualifications are in date.

## Week 3 — Mock audit

- Run an internal **mock inspection** against the four themes that drive most
  483s: data integrity, weak investigations, CAPA without effectiveness, and
  laboratory controls (OOS handling, method validation).
- Practise the **front room / back room** model: who hosts the inspector, who
  retrieves and vets documents.

## Week 4 — Rehearse and rest

- Brief staff on **answering the question asked** — no more, no less; never
  guess.
- Keep a running log of every document provided so answers stay consistent.
- Don't cram the day before. A calm, prepared team beats a frantic one.

## The mindset

Inspection readiness isn't a 30-day project — it's the state a healthy quality
system lives in. These four weeks just surface what's already true.
