# Cell banks: the foundation everything in a biologic rests on

Every batch of a biologic traces back to a vial of cells. If those cells are
consistent, characterised, and free of adventitious agents, you have a foundation to
build on. If they are not, no amount of downstream control will save you. The
**cell bank** is where a biologic's consistency and safety begin.

## Why two tiers: MCB and WCB

Cell banks are built in two tiers, and the reason is practical:

- The **Master Cell Bank (MCB)** is the single, fully characterised origin — created
  once, from a qualified source, and used sparingly.
- The **Working Cell Bank (WCB)** is expanded from the MCB to supply routine
  production, so you do not consume the precious MCB on every campaign.

This protects the original material and gives you a controlled, renewable supply
that all traces back to one characterised source.

## What characterization confirms

Cell-bank characterization is a one-time but extensive effort that establishes:

- **Identity** — the cells are what you say they are
- **Purity / adventitious agents** — freedom from bacteria, fungi, mycoplasma, and
  viruses (a major part of the viral-safety strategy)
- **Genetic stability** — the cell line still produces the intended product, with the
  expected sequence, at the limit of in-vitro cell age used in production

## Why genetic stability is the quiet risk

Cells change as they divide. If the production process pushes the cells beyond the
age at which stability was demonstrated, the product can drift — a subtle shift in
the molecule that downstream tests may not immediately catch. That is why the
**limit of in-vitro cell age** is defined and respected: it bounds the foundation so
the rest of the control strategy holds.
