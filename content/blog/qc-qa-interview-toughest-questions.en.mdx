# The toughest QC/QA interview questions — and how to answer them

A QC/QA interview is not a memory test. Hiring managers already assume you know the
techniques — what they are probing is **judgement under pressure**: how you handle a
failing result, a deviation, or a request to "just release it." Here are the
questions that come up again and again, and how to frame a strong answer.

## 1. "Walk me through how you'd handle an OOS result."

What they want: do you know the **phased** approach and the integrity guardrails.
A strong answer names them — confirm it isn't a calculation error, assess for an
assignable laboratory cause, **never test into compliance**, escalate to a
full-scale investigation if no cause is found, and let QA own the disposition. The
weak answer jumps straight to "retest."

## 2. "Tell me about a deviation you investigated."

What they want: structured root-cause thinking and a **real** systemic fix. Use a
clear structure — what happened, immediate containment, how you got to root cause,
and the corrective **and** preventive actions. Avoid "we retrained the operator" as
the whole answer; that signals you stop at the symptom.

## 3. "How do you think about data integrity?"

What they want: ALCOA+ as a working principle, not a buzzword. Talk about audit-trail
review, unique logins, and treating the record as evidence — and ideally a time you
spotted or closed a gap.

## 4. "What would you do if you were pressured to release a batch with an open issue?"

What they want: backbone. The answer is that an open, batch-impacting deviation or
OOS must be closed first, or the batch goes on hold — and that the releaser's
independence from production is what makes the decision trustworthy. Say it calmly.

## The structure behind every good answer

For behavioural questions, **situation → action → result**, kept concise. For
technical questions, show the **principle** first, then the practice. And it is fine
to say "here's how I'd approach it" rather than inventing a fact — judgement reads
better than a guess.
