# The Contamination Control Strategy: Annex 1's organizing idea

If you read one concept out of the 2022 revision of EU GMP Annex 1, read the
**Contamination Control Strategy (CCS).** It is the organising idea the whole document
hangs on — a deliberate, documented, **holistic** view of how a site controls
microbial, particulate, and endotoxin contamination, and how all those controls work
**together**.

## Not a list — a system

The CCS is not a folder of SOPs. Plenty of sites have gowning procedures, cleaning
records, environmental monitoring, and sterilisation validations — and still have no
CCS, because nobody has stepped back to ask: do these controls, **as a system**, add up
to adequate protection? The CCS is that step back. It maps every control, the risk it
addresses, and crucially the **interactions and gaps** between them.

## What it pulls together

A CCS spans the whole contamination picture, including:

- Facility and **HVAC** design, and the **pressure cascade**
- **Personnel** (gowning, behaviour, training) — the largest risk
- **Cleaning and disinfection**
- **Environmental and process monitoring** (the feedback loop)
- **Sterilisation, depyrogenation, and aseptic process** design
- **Utilities** (water, gases) and raw materials
- **Container closure integrity**

## Why "holistic" is the operative word

The power of the CCS is in seeing the **connections**. Environmental monitoring is not a
standalone test — it is feedback on whether the cleaning, gowning, and air control are
working. A change to the room layout can quietly undermine the airflow a smoke study
validated. The CCS is where those dependencies are made visible, so a change in one
control prompts a look at the others.

## A living document

A CCS is reviewed and updated as the facility, processes, and monitoring data evolve.
Trends feed back into it; changes are assessed against it. Done well, it turns a pile of
individual controls into a coherent, defensible argument that the product will be
sterile — which is exactly what an Annex 1 inspection now expects to see.
