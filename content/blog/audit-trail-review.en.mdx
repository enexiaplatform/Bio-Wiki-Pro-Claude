# Audit Trail Review: What, How Often, and Red Flags

Regulators expect audit trails to be **reviewed**, not just enabled. An unreviewed
audit trail is one of the most cited data-integrity gaps. Here's how to make
review practical and defensible.

## What to review

Audit trails capture who did what, when, and what changed. Focus review on the
entries that carry integrity risk:

- **Changes** to results, methods, integration parameters, or sample sequences.
- **Deletions** and aborted/repeated runs.
- **Changes to system settings** — clock, access rights, audit-trail config.

## How often — be risk-based

You can't manually read every line of every system daily. A defensible model:

- **GMP-critical data** (release-determining results): review the relevant audit
  trail **before** the data is used to make the batch-release decision.
- **Periodic** review of system-level events (configuration, user management) on
  a defined cadence.

The key is a documented, risk-based rationale — not "we review everything"
(unrealistic) and not "we review nothing" (a finding).

## Red flags to watch for

- Results changed after the first acquisition with no explanation.
- "Trial" or unofficial injections immediately before a passing result.
- The system clock altered around the time of a test.
- Audit trail switched off, then on again.
- Repeated aborted runs that never appear in the official record.

## Make it routine

Tie audit-trail review to the batch-record review workflow so it happens before
release — not reconstructed when an inspector asks. Train reviewers on what the
red flags look like in *your* systems.
