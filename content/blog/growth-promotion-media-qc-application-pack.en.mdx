# Growth promotion and media QC application pack

Growth promotion testing is not a generic demonstration that “something grows.” It
is evidence that a defined medium lot, preparation and use condition can perform the
property required by a specific microbiological application.

The application must connect:

```text
medium identity and lot
→ intended method and required property
→ challenge organism and inoculum state
→ preparation, storage and test condition
→ comparator, controls and acceptance rule
→ release, restriction, investigation or rejection
```

## Application boundary

This pack structures planning and review evidence. It does not prescribe organism
panels, inoculum limits, incubation conditions, acceptance criteria, reduced-testing
strategies or expiry periods. Current compendial text, registered methods, approved
procedures and QA/QC decisions remain authoritative.

USP's official FAQ states that growth promotion is checked for each new batch of
medium and distinguishes growth-promoting, inhibitory and indicative-property
conditions. See [USP FAQs for microbial enumeration and specified-organism
tests](https://www.usp.org/frequently-asked-questions/microbial-enumeration-nonsterile-products-tests-specified-microorganisms).
FDA's inspection reference also notes that preparation, overheating and storage can
affect a medium's growth-promotion capability. See [FDA Microbiological
Pharmaceutical Quality Control Labs](https://www.fda.gov/inspections-compliance-enforcement-and-criminal-investigations/inspection-guides/microbiological-pharmaceutical-quality-control-labs-793).

## Define the media-control decision

Create a separate application record for each decision:

| Application | Decision | Typical evidence focus |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Prepared-media batch release | May this internally prepared batch be used? | Formula, preparation cycle, pH, sterility/negative control and required properties |
| Purchased ready-media lot release | May the received lot enter use? | Supplier evidence, transport/receipt, lot identity, local QC strategy and condition |
| Method-specific suitability | Does the medium perform its role in the approved method? | Growth promotion, inhibition, indication/selectivity and method conditions |
| In-use hold | Does performance remain suitable through the approved use period? | Storage, warming, handling, plate/tube condition and end-of-hold challenge |
| Change/comparability | Can a supplier, formulation, format or preparation change be adopted? | Side-by-side performance, method impact, trend continuity and change control |
| Failure investigation | What is the scope and impact of an unacceptable control? | Lot genealogy, preparation, inoculum, incubation, affected tests and disposition |

A certificate review, physical receipt inspection and local performance test answer
different questions. The approved control strategy should state which evidence is
required before use.

## Build the media–method–property matrix

For every medium and format, capture:

- internal and supplier name, code, formulation and lot;
- dehydrated, prepared in-house, ready-to-use or irradiated status;
- plate, tube, bottle, cassette or other format and fill volume;
- applicable test and method step;
- required property: growth-promoting, inhibitory, indicative/selective or sterility;
- challenge organism, strain source and passage governance;
- target inoculum and verification method;
- test and comparator units;
- incubation condition and reading window;
- quantitative or qualitative acceptance rule;
- preparation, release, storage and expiry status;
- owner, reviewer and affected-method list.

One medium may require different properties in different method steps. Do not merge
those applications into a single “GPT passed” status.

## Control receipt and preparation

The record should expose:

- supplier qualification and current specification;
- transport temperature or damage concerns where relevant;
- certificate review and lot reconciliation;
- water grade, weighing, dissolution and pH adjustment;
- heat exposure, sterilization cycle and cooling;
- supplement, antibiotic, neutralizer or indicator addition;
- fill volume, container closure and labeling;
- preparation date, release status, storage condition and expiry;
- visual defects such as dehydration, cracking, contamination, precipitation or
  color change;
- equipment and instrument identifiers used during preparation.

Preparation deviations can change recovery even when the dehydrated-medium lot is
acceptable.

## Govern challenge cultures and inoculum

The challenge system is part of the measurement process. Define:

- reference source, strain identity and traceability;
- receipt, revival, preservation and working-culture hierarchy;
- passage or subculture limit;
- growth phase and preparation condition;
- inoculum target, enumeration/verification method and timing;
- diluent, hold time, temperature and mixing;
- purity and identity checks;
- allowed use window and invalidation conditions;
- reconciliation of prepared doses and unused material.

An assumed inoculum cannot support a quantitative recovery comparison. Record the
verified inoculum result and its relationship to the tested units.

## Separate property-test architectures

### Growth-promoting property

Define inoculated unit count, inoculum level, comparator, incubation window, colony
or turbidity reading, recovery calculation and acceptance rule. Quantitative solid
media and qualitative liquid media should not share an invented universal rule.

### Inhibitory property

Define the organism being inhibited, challenge level, minimum incubation condition,
expected absence or suppression, and rules for atypical breakthrough growth.

### Indicative or selective property

Define expected colony appearance, reaction, color, morphology or confirmatory
step, including how ambiguous reactions are handled.

### Negative or sterility control

Define uninoculated units, incubation exposure, observation, contamination response
and the relationship to released and already-used media.

## Design comparators and replication explicitly

Record:

- reference or previously approved comparator lot;
- comparator storage and remaining shelf life;
- number of units and independent preparations;
- plate position or handling controls;
- calculation of recovery ratio where applicable;
- qualitative comparability criteria;
- treatment of zero, spreader, confluent or contaminated units;
- invalid-test versus true-failure rules.

Comparator choice can dominate the result. A poorly stored reference lot must not
be treated as unquestioned truth.

## Build the release decision

The release record should answer:

1. Were identity, receipt and preparation records acceptable?
2. Were instruments, incubation and challenge cultures in control?
3. Was the inoculum verified and within the approved condition?
4. Did every required property meet its application-specific criterion?
5. Were negative controls acceptable?
6. Were deviations, atypical observations and calculations reviewed?
7. Which methods and expiry period are authorized?
8. Who released the lot and when did usable status begin?

Partial release or restricted use requires an explicit method/property scope; a lot
must not appear universally approved when only one application was demonstrated.

## Investigate failures by genealogy and impact

Trace a failure across:

- supplier and dehydrated/ready-media lot;
- preparation batch, sterilization load and operator;
- water, supplements and containers;
- challenge-culture preparation and inoculum verification;
- incubator, location, timing and excursion;
- comparator lot and calculation;
- other organisms/properties tested;
- product, water or environmental tests already performed with the affected media;
- retained units and repeat rationale;
- supplier notification, CAPA and effectiveness evidence.

Repeating only the failed organism without a documented hypothesis does not explain
the original result or establish product-test impact.

## Convert media QC into resource demand

For every lot or preparation batch, model:

- receipt, quarantine and certificate-review time;
- preparation, sterilization, dispensing and labeling labor;
- pH meter, balance, autoclave and dispensing-equipment occupancy;
- challenge-culture revival, inoculum preparation and verification;
- test and comparator plates/tubes by organism and property;
- incubator positions and unit-days by condition;
- scheduled reading windows and weekend coverage;
- calculation, technical review and release time;
- retained media and storage footprint;
- failure, repeat, investigation and affected-test review scenarios;
- waste, decontamination and stockout contingency.

Forecast by media lot and preparation cadence, not only by number of product tests.
A supplier minimum order or short shelf life can create more QC demand than the
nominal consumption forecast suggests.

## Lifecycle and reduced-testing governance

Review the application when changing supplier, formulation, format, preparation
cycle, supplement, container, storage, expiry, organism source, inoculum method,
incubator, acceptance calculation or intended test.

Any reduced or skip-testing strategy should have:

- defined eligible media/application scope;
- supplier and incoming-history evidence;
- ongoing certificate and physical checks;
- periodic local challenge frequency;
- failure and change triggers that restore full testing;
- trend review and governance owner;
- documented impact on inventory quarantine and continuity.

Atlas records the strategy and its evidence status. It does not infer that purchased
media or a supplier certificate automatically permits reduced local testing.

## Evidence required for an executable Atlas node

1. Media–method–property matrix.
2. Approved receipt/preparation and release workflow.
3. Controlled organism and inoculum genealogy.
4. Property-specific method, controls and calculations.
5. Comparator and acceptance configuration.
6. Storage, expiry and in-use hold evidence.
7. Failure, affected-use and disposition pathway.
8. Observed BOM, equipment, incubation and analyst demand.
9. Supplier/change/reduced-testing governance.
10. Estimate-to-actual records across representative lots and failures.
