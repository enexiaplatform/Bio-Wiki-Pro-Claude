# Microbiology reference-culture lifecycle and capacity planning

A reference culture is not a generic consumable. Its source, identity, genealogy,
passage, preparation, storage and intended application affect the credibility of
growth promotion, method suitability, positive controls and other microbiology work.

```text
method and organism requirement
→ qualified source and receipt
→ revival and master/stock hierarchy
→ controlled passage and working preparation
→ application-specific inoculum
→ reconciliation, storage and disposal
→ performance review and replenishment
```

Atlas can structure the register and resource demand. It does not select organisms,
set passage limits, approve equivalence, establish inoculum targets or authorize the
use of a culture in a particular method.

## Build an organism–application matrix

For every controlled organism, record:

- taxonomic name and controlled identifier;
- collection, supplier and catalog/strain reference;
- cited or equivalence basis in the current method;
- intended applications and required property;
- media, incubation and recovery conditions;
- quantitative or qualitative inoculum need;
- biosafety and containment basis;
- source certificate and receipt lot;
- lifecycle owner, reviewer and approved status;
- alternate source and change-control requirement.

USP's official [FAQs for microbial enumeration and specified-organism
tests](https://www.usp.org/frequently-asked-questions/microbial-enumeration-nonsterile-products-tests-specified-microorganisms)
state that cited strains or equivalent strains from other culture collections should
be used for the applications discussed there. The site must determine equivalence and
applicability against its current controlled method.

## Design the culture hierarchy

Define the approved hierarchy, for example:

```text
received reference unit
→ primary or master stock
→ working stock
→ application culture
→ inoculum dose
```

For every transition capture:

- parent identifier and passage/generation relationship;
- date, operator and controlled procedure;
- medium and incubation condition;
- purity and identity checks;
- preservation method and container;
- storage location and expiry/retest status;
- number of units created and reconciled;
- permitted downstream applications;
- deviation or atypical observation.

The exact hierarchy and passage accounting must come from the approved program. Atlas
should expose inconsistencies, not invent a universal convention.

## Control receipt and revival

The receipt workflow should include:

- package and temperature condition;
- lot, strain and document reconciliation;
- storage deadline and location;
- revival medium and condition;
- recovery, purity and expected-characteristic observation;
- identity confirmation strategy;
- failed-revival or mixed-culture pathway;
- source complaint or replacement;
- release for stock preparation.

Do not allow a received unit to become available for routine use before its required
receipt and revival evidence is reviewed.

## Preserve passage and genealogy

The record must allow a reviewer to trace an application dose back to its received
source. Capture:

- passage definition used by the site;
- each subculture event;
- parent and child identifiers;
- count or generation at each event;
- allowed maximum and current status;
- prohibited further propagation;
- exceptional use and approval;
- disposal or retirement.

Avoid reconstructing genealogy from freezer-box labels after a failure. The data must
exist at the time each transition occurs.

## Separate stock preparation from inoculum preparation

Stock creation supports continuity. Application inoculum supports a specific test.
For the latter define:

- intended method, medium property or recovery study;
- culture age and growth phase;
- diluent and preparation sequence;
- target dose and verification method;
- timing between preparation, verification and inoculation;
- mixing and hold condition;
- dose units prepared, used and discarded;
- purity and control observations;
- invalidation and repeat rules.

An assumed inoculum does not support a quantitative recovery comparison. Record the
verified result and how it applies to the tested units.

## Model inventory and expiry by application

Forecast:

- organisms required by method and media portfolio;
- receipt and revival lead time;
- master/working units per preparation;
- routine application doses;
- GPT, suitability, training and investigation demand;
- failed revival, contamination and verification loss;
- expiry and remaining shelf life;
- freezer or refrigerator positions;
- minimum reserve and replenishment trigger;
- alternate supplier or stock-recovery plan.

Do not multiply “organisms × tests” without the approved stock and dose architecture.
One working preparation may support several doses, but timing and hold restrictions
can prevent perfect consolidation.

## Convert culture control into workload

Include:

- ordering, receipt and certificate review;
- revival and incubation;
- purity and identity testing;
- stock preparation, labeling and storage;
- inoculum preparation and enumeration/verification;
- scheduled observations and readings;
- inventory reconciliation;
- equipment and controlled-workspace occupancy;
- media, diluent, containers and disposal;
- technical review and release;
- failed, contaminated or atypical-culture investigations;
- training and periodic qualification.

Reference-culture work can cluster around media lots, suitability campaigns and
stock expiry even when routine product testing is stable.

## Protect storage and continuity

For each storage condition define:

- qualified equipment and location;
- box, rack and position mapping;
- access and inventory control;
- monitoring, alarm and escalation;
- temperature-excursion assessment;
- backup unit and transfer route;
- separation or security requirements;
- maximum tolerable downtime;
- duplicate stock strategy where approved;
- disaster recovery and supplier lead time.

Two boxes in the same freezer are not independent continuity. Two freezers on the
same circuit or in the same unmonitored room can share a common-cause failure.

## Govern changes and atypical performance

Review impact when changing source, strain reference, medium, revival method, stock
architecture, preservation, container, storage, passage convention, inoculum method,
verification technique or intended application.

Investigate repeated poor recovery, changed morphology, contamination, discordant
identity, inoculum instability or unexplained application failure across the affected
genealogy. Preserve links to media lots, methods, equipment and tests already
performed.

## Evidence package before program approval

Collect:

1. organism–application matrix;
2. source, strain and equivalence evidence;
3. approved hierarchy and passage convention;
4. receipt, revival, purity and identity workflow;
5. parent–child genealogy and stock records;
6. application inoculum and verification method;
7. inventory, expiry and replenishment model;
8. storage, monitoring and backup evidence;
9. BOM, analyst, workspace and incubation demand;
10. failure, change and impact-assessment pathway;
11. roles, training and review status;
12. actual recovery, loss, usage and replenishment history.

## What Atlas should block

Atlas should not label culture readiness complete when:

- strain source or method applicability is unresolved;
- equivalence is asserted without controlled evidence;
- passage/generation convention is undefined;
- application doses cannot trace to the received source;
- stock and inoculum preparation are merged;
- verified inoculum evidence is absent where required;
- expiry, reserve and supplier lead time are missing;
- storage capacity and alarm response are assumed;
- common-cause failure affects all stocks;
- actual recovery and usage cannot be calibrated.
