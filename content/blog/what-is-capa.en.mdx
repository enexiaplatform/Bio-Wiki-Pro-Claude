# What Is CAPA? Corrective and Preventive Action Explained

CAPA — **Corrective and Preventive Action** — is how a quality system learns from
problems. It's one of the most scrutinised processes in any GMP inspection, and
also one of the most misunderstood.

## Three terms people mix up

- **Correction** — the immediate fix. (Reject the batch, clean the spill.)
- **Corrective action** — eliminates the *cause* of a problem that already
  happened, so it doesn't recur.
- **Preventive action** — eliminates the cause of a *potential* problem before it
  happens.

A correction is not a CAPA. "We re-trained the operator" is not a corrective
action unless you've shown training was the root cause.

## Root cause comes first

The single most common CAPA finding is jumping to actions before establishing the
root cause. Use a structured tool — **5 Whys** or an **Ishikawa (fishbone)**
diagram — and tie every action to a verified cause.

## The effectiveness check (the part everyone skips)

A CAPA isn't closed when the action is done — it's closed when you've **proven the
action worked**. Define quantitative, time-bound criteria *before* closing:
e.g. "zero recurrence across the next 3 batches." Objective data, not "training
completed."

## Why auditors care

CAPA is the engine of continuous improvement (ICH Q10). A weak CAPA system means
problems keep recurring — which is exactly what regulators are trying to prevent.
