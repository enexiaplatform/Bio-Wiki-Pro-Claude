# Controlled Quality Lab Blueprint acceptance and handover

A Blueprint is useful when decision-makers can see what it covers, what evidence it
uses, what remains uncertain and which actions transfer to them. A polished document
without a controlled acceptance basis can be mistaken for a validated design or lose
its assumptions during implementation.

```text
approved project question and input baseline
→ compiled recommendations and scenarios
→ evidence, assumptions and limitations
→ client/expert review and disposition
→ controlled release and acceptance
→ action, ownership and implementation handover
→ revision and estimate-to-actual learning
```

Atlas can produce and govern a decision-support package. It does not certify a
laboratory design, approve regulatory compliance, accept risk for the client or
replace qualified engineering, QA, finance, procurement and legal decisions.

## Freeze the decision baseline

The released package should identify:

- project, site, owner and decision purpose;
- products, markets, volumes and planning horizon;
- methods, capabilities and in-house/outsource allocation;
- input dataset and freeze date;
- Domain Pack, rule and evidence versions;
- scenarios and selected basis;
- currency, location and cost base date;
- turnaround, shifts and operating calendar;
- exclusions and interfaces;
- report revision and release status.

Later input changes must not silently rewrite the basis used for the original
decision.

## Maintain an assumptions and evidence register

For every material assumption record:

- unique identifier and statement;
- supplied fact, derived value, rule or placeholder status;
- source and version;
- applicability conditions;
- confidence and uncertainty;
- affected outputs;
- consequence if wrong;
- owner and reviewer;
- action required to verify or close;
- due date and final disposition.

Separate evidence absence from conservative choice. A conservative factor may reduce
one risk while increasing cost, space or staffing.

## Distinguish recommendation maturity

Use explicit maturity labels such as:

- concept scenario;
- evidence collection basis;
- expert-review candidate;
- budgetary planning basis;
- engineering/procurement input after qualified review;
- excluded from decision pending evidence.

The label should follow the weakest critical dependency. A detailed spreadsheet does
not become procurement-ready when method applicability or utility conditions remain
unreviewed.

## Build a decision and exception log

Record:

- decision question;
- options considered;
- selected option and rationale;
- evidence and assumptions used;
- affected cost, schedule, capacity and risk;
- dissent, condition or limitation;
- decision owner and date;
- implementation action;
- change/review trigger.

Also preserve rejected, deferred and unresolved options. Future reviewers need to
understand why a recommendation was selected, not only what the final table says.

## Define acceptance before final review

Acceptance criteria can include:

- agreed scope and deliverables present;
- required source and assumption traceability;
- calculation and scenario reconciliation;
- critical gaps visibly classified;
- named expert/functional reviews completed;
- client corrections dispositioned;
- confidentiality and data-use controls met;
- permitted-use statement and limitations included;
- action and ownership register complete;
- revision, release and archive controls applied.

Acceptance confirms delivery against agreed criteria. It does not automatically
approve every recommendation or verify a Domain Pack.

## Separate review, approval and acknowledgement

Define what each signature or status means:

- **prepared by:** assembled the package;
- **technical review:** evaluated assigned technical scope;
- **quality/regulatory review:** assessed relevant governance boundaries;
- **commercial/financial review:** reviewed cost and commercial assumptions;
- **client acceptance:** confirms contracted deliverable acceptance;
- **risk acceptance:** authorized owner accepts a stated residual risk;
- **implementation approval:** authorizes a downstream project action.

One signature must not be interpreted as all of these. Atlas should store the role,
scope, date, conditions and evidence reference.

## Control corrections and open items

For each review comment capture:

- identifier, author and date;
- affected section, rule, input or output;
- comment and requested action;
- response and evidence;
- accepted, rejected, deferred or superseded disposition;
- resulting change and revision;
- approver and closure date.

Open items need a severity, owner, due date and effect on permitted use. A report may
be released with open actions only when the release authority accepts the stated
boundary.

## Create the handover package

Handover should include:

- controlled Blueprint and decision brief;
- input and scenario baseline;
- capability, method and resource maps;
- evidence and assumptions register;
- risk, gap and action register;
- URS/RFQ or procurement inputs where in scope;
- cost, schedule and expansion assumptions;
- data dictionary and calculation notes;
- revision and review history;
- client-owned source files and access instructions;
- confidentiality, retention and permitted-use terms;
- next review, expiry or change triggers.

Assign each action to an accountable organization and role. “Client to confirm” is
not sufficient ownership.

## Protect knowledge across the lifecycle

ICH Q10 describes knowledge management across development, technology transfer,
commercial manufacturing and discontinuation. See the official [ICH Q10
Pharmaceutical Quality System](https://database.ich.org/sites/default/files/Q10_Guideline.pdf).

For Atlas, retain reusable learning only when:

- client confidentiality and rights permit it;
- project-specific facts are separated from general rules;
- the frozen estimate and actual observation share aligned scope;
- evidence provenance and reviewer interpretation are preserved;
- a rule change follows controlled cross-case review;
- a new Domain Pack version is separately approved.

Client acceptance does not turn project data into an Atlas benchmark automatically.

## Define revision and expiry triggers

Reopen or supersede the package when:

- product, market, volume or shift changes;
- method, specification or applicability changes;
- new site evidence closes a critical assumption;
- supplier/configuration or utility basis changes;
- cost/lead-time evidence becomes stale;
- actual workload materially differs;
- expert review changes a recommendation;
- a risk, deviation or investigation changes the basis;
- implementation moves to a higher decision maturity.

Preserve prior releases. Do not overwrite the package used for an earlier decision.

## Evidence package before controlled release

Collect:

1. scope, purpose and input freeze;
2. versioned rules, evidence and calculations;
3. assumptions, gaps and limitations;
4. scenario and recommendation rationale;
5. functional/expert review records;
6. correction and comment dispositions;
7. acceptance criteria and permitted use;
8. roles, signatures and decision meanings;
9. residual risks and action ownership;
10. handover inventory and access;
11. revision, expiry and change triggers;
12. confidentiality and learning-use controls.

## What Atlas should block

Atlas should not label a Blueprint controlled or accepted when:

- scope and input baseline can change silently;
- evidence, rules or assumptions are unversioned;
- recommendation maturity is unstated;
- critical gaps are hidden in narrative;
- review comments lack disposition;
- signatures have ambiguous meaning;
- client acceptance is presented as regulatory or technical approval;
- actions have no accountable owner;
- confidential project data can flow into public/reusable assets without rights;
- prior releases can be overwritten.

## Apply this in Atlas

Compile scenarios in the [Quality Lab Planner](/quality-lab/planner), resolve evidence
through the [Review Workspace](/quality-lab/review), inspect governance history in
[Governance History](/quality-lab/governance-history) and capture approved learning
candidates in the [Calibration Learning Review Queue](/quality-lab/calibration). Use
the controlled delivery package only within its stated maturity and permitted use.

This guide is decision support. It does not replace contracted acceptance terms,
document control, client risk authority, QA approval, regulatory review, qualified
engineering, procurement authorization or legal advice.
