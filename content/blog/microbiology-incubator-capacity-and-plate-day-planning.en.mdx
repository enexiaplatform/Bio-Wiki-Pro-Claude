# Microbiology incubator capacity and plate-day planning

An incubator is not sized by chamber volume or total plate count. Microbiology
work occupies qualified positions for different durations, at different conditions,
with fixed reading windows and imperfect arrival patterns.

The planning chain is:

```text
physical method executions
→ containers by incubation condition
→ occupied position-days
→ arrival, reading and removal calendar
→ qualified usable rack geometry
→ peak utilization and excursion exposure
→ quantity, backup and expansion decision
```

Atlas can structure this calculation and its evidence. It does not set incubation
conditions, approve mixed loads, establish qualified loading patterns or determine
the disposition of material exposed to an excursion.

## Define the incubation application

Create a separate demand stream for each method branch and approved condition.
Capture:

- product, water, environmental, media-QC, suitability or investigation use;
- physical execution count and arrival pattern;
- plate, tube, bottle, bag or other container format;
- number of units per execution, including controls;
- incubation temperature or other controlled condition;
- minimum, target and maximum duration;
- staged or sequential incubation and transfer rule;
- reading, observation or subculture windows;
- segregation, containment or dedicated-use requirement;
- post-reading hold, quarantine and investigation rules;
- method and evidence version, owner and review status.

“Room temperature,” “warm” and “cold” are not usable planning conditions. Use the
approved method's actual control band and lifecycle status.

## Use position-days, not just plate counts

For a simple demand stream:

```text
position-days
= physical executions
× containers per execution
× occupied days
× approved repeat or exception allowance
```

Calculate separately by condition and container format. Ten bottles can consume more
rack height than many plates. Stacked plates may have a different qualified loading
pattern from single plates. Tubes and bags require their actual holder geometry.

Keep routine controls explicit. Growth-promotion units, negative controls,
suitability challenges and investigation holds are method demand, not unexplained
waste.

## Convert internal dimensions into qualified usable positions

Nominal shelf area is not usable capacity. Build a geometry record containing:

- chamber and shelf internal dimensions;
- number and adjustable range of shelves;
- container footprint, height and holder footprint;
- approved stacking and spacing;
- airflow clearances and mapped restrictions;
- door, fan, sensor and drain obstructions;
- zones excluded by qualification or operating practice;
- maximum and minimum qualified loading patterns;
- separation between methods, materials or status;
- safe handling and reading access;
- evidence reference and configuration version.

Use a physical fit check or controlled layout drawing. Do not derive capacity by
dividing shelf area by a perfect container footprint and assuming every remainder is
usable.

## Preserve condition and program separation

Different temperatures, atmospheres, light conditions or program requirements are
not automatically interchangeable. Even at the same nominal temperature, the site
may require separation for:

- clean media versus test samples;
- product versus environmental samples;
- routine work versus investigations;
- challenge cultures or positive controls;
- released versus quarantined status;
- organism or biosafety considerations;
- compendial, registered or validated method versions;
- different reading or staged-incubation practices.

Record whether separation is physical, procedural, temporal or unnecessary under an
approved risk basis.

## Place demand on a calendar

Monthly plate-days can conceal a daily collision. Build a daily or shift-level view
when turnaround or utilization is material. Include:

- routine batch release arrivals;
- fixed water and environmental-monitoring rounds;
- media preparation and growth-promotion cadence;
- method-suitability campaigns;
- stability pulls;
- weekend and holiday readings;
- launch, validation and qualification studies;
- invalid, repeat and investigation loads;
- post-incubation holds pending review or identification.

Track the time containers enter, each required reading, any transfer between
conditions and the earliest permitted removal. A five-day duration does not always
mean five exact calendar days of occupancy if loading and reading windows differ.

## Convert calendar capacity into usable capacity

Keep each reduction visible:

```text
usable position-days
= qualified positions
× available calendar days
× scheduled availability
× packing/operating efficiency
× approved planning-utilization limit
```

Scheduled availability accounts for mapping, calibration, maintenance, cleaning and
planned shutdown. Packing efficiency reflects real geometry and arrival mismatch.
Planning utilization preserves reviewed headroom for variability, urgent work and
recovery.

These factors are project assumptions until supported by observed data. Do not hide
them in one generic utilization percentage.

## Model door openings and reading practice

The capacity model should state whether plates are read:

- in situ through a viewing arrangement;
- removed and returned;
- removed permanently at the first reading;
- transferred to another condition;
- moved to a temporary staging area;
- held pending confirmation or investigation.

Door openings, large transfers and extended readings may affect temperature recovery,
workflow time and contamination risk. The site must define operating and qualification
evidence for its practice.

## Plan excursion and failure scenarios

For each incubator and demand stream, capture:

- alarm points, delay and notification route;
- independent monitoring and record access;
- maximum tolerable interruption or excursion;
- assessment owner and required data;
- qualified alternate capacity and available free positions;
- transfer time, route and container controls;
- generator or utility dependency;
- repair and service lead time;
- backlog growth during downtime;
- recovery rate after return to service;
- common-cause failures across apparent backups.

The backup must have the correct condition, geometry, qualification and available
capacity. An empty chamber at another temperature is not automatically a backup.

## Reconcile capacity with data and qualification

The FDA's non-binding [Guide to Inspections of Microbiological Pharmaceutical
Quality Control Laboratories](https://www.fda.gov/inspections-compliance-enforcement-and-criminal-investigations/inspection-guides/microbiological-pharmaceutical-quality-control-labs-793)
emphasizes review of incubated plates and tubes against recorded analyses and the
approved analytical procedure. EU GMP Annex 15 is available through the European
Commission's official [EudraLex Volume 4](https://health.ec.europa.eu/medicinal-products/eudralex/eudralex-volume-4_en)
and provides the broader qualification lifecycle context.

Relevant evidence may include mapping and qualification protocols, sensor
calibration, alarm challenges, recovery studies, loading patterns, maintenance,
temperature records, deviations, and method-specific incubation procedures.

## Evidence package before quantity freeze

Collect:

1. physical executions by method and condition;
2. container/control count per execution;
3. approved duration, reading and transfer rules;
4. container and holder geometry;
5. qualified usable loading pattern;
6. daily arrivals and peak scenarios;
7. scheduled downtime and operating calendar;
8. historical excursions, failures and repair times;
9. alternate-capacity and transfer evidence;
10. alarm, monitoring and assessment workflow;
11. future demand and procurement triggers;
12. estimate-to-actual position and utilization records.

## What Atlas should block

Atlas should not present incubator quantity as review-ready when:

- total plates are used without duration and condition;
- regulatory rows have not been reconciled to physical executions;
- controls, suitability, GPT or investigations are omitted;
- container and rack geometry are assumed;
- nominal volume or shelf area is treated as qualified capacity;
- arrival and reading collisions are hidden by monthly averages;
- downtime, mapping and maintenance are ignored;
- backup condition, qualification or free capacity is unverified;
- excursion assessment and data ownership are unclear;
- no future trigger or calibration plan exists.
