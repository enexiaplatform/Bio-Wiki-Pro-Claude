# What Is a Certificate of Analysis (CoA)?

A **Certificate of Analysis (CoA)** is the document that summarizes the test
results for a specific batch of material or product and states whether it meets
its specification. It's the quality "passport" that travels with a batch.

## What a CoA contains

- **Product / material name and batch (lot) number.**
- Each **test** performed, with its **specification (acceptance criterion)** and
  the **actual result**.
- The **method/pharmacopoeial reference** used (e.g. USP, EP).
- A **pass/fail (conforms) conclusion** and the **release decision** with the
  authorized signatory and date.
- Manufacturing and expiry/retest dates.

## Why it matters

The CoA is how a buyer (or the next step in manufacturing) trusts an incoming
material without re-testing everything. It links three things: the **spec**, the
**result**, and the **disposition**. If those don't line up, the CoA is
worthless — which is why data integrity behind it matters as much as the document.

## CoA vs CoC

A **Certificate of Conformance (CoC)** attests that a product was made per
requirements but may not list actual results. A **CoA** gives the actual numbers.
For incoming-material control, many programs verify the supplier's CoA against
their own periodic testing.

## The QC takeaway

A CoA is only as good as the calibrated instruments, validated methods, qualified
analysts, and intact data behind each number on it.
