# Bacterial endotoxins BET/LAL application pack

The Bacterial Endotoxins Test (BET) is an application chain, not a standalone reader
run. A defensible result connects the product and patient-use basis to an approved
endotoxin limit, sample concentration, maximum valid dilution (MVD), suitable test
method, interference controls and controlled interpretation.

```text
article and intended use
→ approved endotoxin limit basis
→ sample concentration and MVD
→ preparation and dilution
→ method, reagent and sensitivity
→ interfering-factors evidence and controls
→ valid result and product/process decision
```

## Specialist-gated boundary

This pack structures evidence discovery and laboratory-capability planning. It does
not establish K or M, calculate a registered product specification, approve an
endotoxin limit, select a method for a product, validate a recombinant alternative,
interpret an invalid run or make a batch-release decision.

Use current monographs, registrations, compendial chapters, approved procedures and
qualified BET/QA review. USP's official pages describe the established chapter
[&lt;85&gt; Bacterial Endotoxins Test](https://doi.usp.org/USPNF/USPNF_M98830_02_01.html)
and additional recombinant techniques in [&lt;86&gt;](https://doi.usp.org/USPNF/USPNF_M16015_02_01.html).
Applicability and alternative-method obligations must be assessed for the specific
article and market.

## Define the intended decision

Create a separate application record for each use:

| Application | Decision | Evidence focus |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Finished-product release | Does the batch comply with its approved endotoxin requirement? | Registered/compendial limit, product concentration, suitable routine method and valid controls |
| In-process control | Is endotoxin controlled at a process stage? | Process purpose, sampling state, internal limit/trend and downstream clearance assumptions |
| Water or utility monitoring | Does the utility meet its defined chemical/microbial control requirement? | Water type/use, sample point, hold, limit and method suitability |
| Raw material/component | Is the input acceptable for its intended process/product use? | Material risk, sampling/extraction, limit allocation and interference |
| Medical-device extract | Does the defined extraction and device application meet the applicable requirement? | Device category, extraction ratio/conditions and applicable standards |
| Method transfer/change | Can the method, reagent, platform or site change be adopted? | Comparability, interference, validation/verification and regulatory impact |
| Investigation | What does an adverse, atypical or invalid signal imply? | Original data, controls, sample genealogy, process context and product impact |

Do not reuse a calculation simply because product names look similar. Concentration,
dose, route, presentation and registered requirements can change the application.

## Build the article–limit–method matrix

Capture:

- article, strength, presentation and dosage form;
- patient route and maximum dose basis;
- market, monograph, registration and internal specification sources;
- approved K and M terms or other applicable limit basis;
- calculated and approved endotoxin limit with unit;
- sample/product concentration used in the test;
- reagent sensitivity or lowest standard concentration;
- MVD calculation, unit reconciliation and approved working dilution range;
- product preparation, extraction or pooling;
- gel-clot, kinetic chromogenic, endpoint chromogenic, kinetic turbidimetric,
  endpoint turbidimetric or approved recombinant architecture;
- reagent manufacturer, lot and instrument/software;
- interfering-factors/suitability status;
- routine control scheme, calculation and reportable result;
- owner, reviewer, version and change history.

The matrix should preserve every input and unit. A calculator output without its
approved source chain is not a specification.

## Control the endotoxin-limit chain

Document the source and interpretation of every term:

```text
K = allowable pyrogenic dose basis under the applicable requirement
M = maximum recommended dose per kilogram or other defined dose basis
endotoxin limit = approved relationship of K and M, where applicable
MVD = relationship among endotoxin limit, sample concentration and method sensitivity
```

Verify:

- dose route, frequency and time basis;
- adult/pediatric or special-population considerations in the approved basis;
- concentration versus total-container dose;
- unit consistency across EU, mL, mg, unit or device;
- reconstitution and administration condition;
- pooling or composite implications;
- rounding and significant figures;
- relationship between calculated limit and registered specification;
- independent technical and QA review.

The [Endotoxin Limit Calculator](/tools/endotoxin-limit-calculator) is a review aid.
It must not silently supply missing regulatory or product facts.

## Treat MVD as a constrained working space

MVD describes the greatest dilution at which the applicable endotoxin limit can still
be detected under the defined method sensitivity and sample concentration. It does
not prove that every dilution up to MVD is suitable.

For each working dilution, consider:

- product solubility and homogeneity;
- pH, ionic strength, chelators, surfactants and proteins;
- color, turbidity or fluorescence;
- adsorption to vessels or dilution materials;
- endotoxin masking or low endotoxin recovery concerns;
- dilution precision and minimum sample volume;
- spike recovery or positive product control behavior;
- routine robustness and analyst handling;
- margin between working dilution and MVD.

Select a routine dilution from demonstrated suitability, not from MVD alone.

## Control sample collection and preparation

Define:

- endotoxin-controlled container, closure, tips, tubes and water;
- sampling point, state and representative quantity;
- transport, hold time and temperature;
- reconstitution, extraction, mixing and equilibration;
- dilution sequence, vessels and pipetting scheme;
- heat, pH adjustment or other approved treatment;
- adsorption, carryover and contamination precautions;
- sample pooling and traceability;
- preparation replicates and allowed hold;
- invalid preparation and repeat restrictions.

“Endotoxin-free” should be supported by the controlled material specification and
use condition; it is not a casual label.

## Separate the method architectures

### Gel-clot limit test

Define labeled reagent sensitivity, confirmation, sample and positive-product-control
tubes, incubation condition, undisturbed handling, endpoint reading and valid test
logic. Preserve the qualitative nature of the result.

### Gel-clot quantitative test

Define dilution series, endpoint determination, replicate rules, geometric mean and
validity criteria under the approved procedure.

### Kinetic chromogenic or turbidimetric methods

Define standard curve, reaction time/rate signal, reader configuration, temperature,
replicates, blank, product control, curve criteria, spike recovery, calculation and
out-of-range handling.

### Endpoint photometric methods

Define reaction timing, stopping condition, wavelength, blank/color correction,
standard curve and interference controls.

### Recombinant reagent methods

Define rFC or recombinant cascade architecture, applicable chapter/alternative-method
status, bridging or validation strategy, instrument and software, product suitability,
comparability evidence and regulatory/change-control pathway. Do not assume that
operational similarity removes method-lifecycle obligations.

## Demonstrate interfering-factors suitability

The study should use the actual article, preparation, method, reagent and proposed
dilutions. Capture:

- product lots/strengths and worst-case rationale;
- dilution range within MVD;
- positive product control/endotoxin spike preparation;
- inhibition or enhancement outcome;
- pH and optical/interference observations;
- reagent lot and reference standard traceability;
- replicate, analyst, day and equipment coverage as approved;
- acceptance, invalidity and repeat rules;
- grouping/bracketing rationale;
- routine dilution and fallback dilution;
- change triggers requiring reassessment.

Passing water controls does not demonstrate that the product matrix is suitable.

## Build controls into every run and the BOM

Depending on the approved architecture, include:

- reference-standard or control-standard endotoxin;
- reagent sensitivity/standard curve controls;
- negative water/reagent blank;
- positive product control;
- sample replicates;
- endotoxin-controlled water, tips, tubes and plates;
- instrument calibration/performance checks;
- temperature and timing controls;
- analyst qualification and periodic proficiency;
- reagent-lot qualification or bridging;
- contamination, spill and decontamination controls.

Controls are part of the method's cost and capacity. They must not be hidden in a
generic consumable-waste allowance.

## Define run validity before sample interpretation

The controlled decision sequence should answer:

1. Were reagent, reference standard, equipment and environmental conditions valid?
2. Did the sensitivity confirmation or standard curve meet requirements?
3. Was the negative control acceptable?
4. Did the positive product control demonstrate absence of unacceptable interference?
5. Were replicate and dilution criteria satisfied?
6. Was the result within the reportable range and below the applicable MVD?
7. Were calculations, units and transcription independently reviewed?
8. Only then: what product or process decision does the valid result support?

An invalid test is not a passing product result, and a repeat does not erase the
original invalidity or atypical observation.

## Investigate invalid, atypical and adverse results

Preserve and review:

- sample identity, collection, hold and preparation;
- dilution genealogy and pipetting records;
- reagent, standard, water and consumable lots;
- instrument, software, temperature and timing;
- raw curve, reaction-time or gel observations;
- controls and positive-product-control behavior;
- analyst qualification and concurrent runs;
- laboratory contamination and carryover hypotheses;
- product/process history and related samples;
- repeat or resample rationale established before testing;
- product impact and QA disposition.

The investigation should distinguish contamination, inhibition/enhancement,
calculation/configuration error, sample non-representativeness and a genuine process
signal.

## Connect BET to process and contamination control

The test does not remove endotoxin. Interpret results alongside:

- raw-material and water controls;
- equipment cleaning and depyrogenation;
- microbial growth/death opportunities;
- hold times and temperatures;
- filtration and purification steps;
- bioburden trends and Gram-negative organism signals;
- container/component controls;
- process deviations and maintenance;
- validated clearance or reduction claims;
- upstream/downstream sample relationships.

FDA's current [Pyrogen and Endotoxins Testing Q&A](https://www.fda.gov/regulatory-information/search-fda-guidance-documents/pyrogen-and-endotoxins-testing-questions-and-answers)
provides current agency recommendations across drugs, biological products and
devices. Apply the relevant product-specific framework rather than treating all
articles alike.

## Convert the application into resource demand

Model by method and product matrix:

- sample receipt, preparation and dilution time;
- standards, controls and positive-product-control preparation;
- gel tubes or microplate wells per sample/dilution;
- reagent, water, tips, tubes, plates and endotoxin-controlled accessories;
- reader/incubator occupancy and fixed timing windows;
- standard-curve and control overhead per run;
- calculation, raw-data and second-person review;
- suitability, transfer, reagent-lot and analyst-qualification demand;
- invalid, repeat, investigation and expedited-release scenarios;
- cold storage and reagent expiry/wastage;
- software, calibration, service and backup-reader continuity;
- after-hours, weekend and short-hold samples.

Batch samples intelligently only when hold time, turnaround and run controls permit.
A nominal samples-per-plate count is not usable capacity if the product requires
multiple dilutions and controls.

## Reagent, platform and continuity strategy

Capture:

- approved supplier and reagent architecture;
- lot reservation, lead time, storage, shelf life and shipment conditions;
- reference/control standard traceability;
- reader and software compatibility;
- backup equipment and alternate reagent/site qualification;
- single-source and animal-derived/recombinant transition risks;
- supplier change notification;
- method-transfer and regulatory impact;
- critical consumable safety stock and expiry exposure.

Continuity alternatives must be qualified before a shortage, not selected during a
release emergency.

## Lifecycle and change control

Reassess the application when product formulation, strength, dose, route,
reconstitution, specification, sample concentration, preparation, working dilution,
reagent, sensitivity, method architecture, reader, software, standard, consumable,
supplier, laboratory site or applicable chapter changes.

Trace each change to limit/MVD, interference, validation/verification, registered
commitments, historical comparability, training, BOM, capacity and electronic
configuration.

## Evidence required before an executable Atlas node

1. Approved article–limit–method application matrix.
2. Traceable K/M or alternative limit basis and unit review.
3. Sample concentration, MVD and justified working dilution.
4. Controlled preparation and method architecture.
5. Product-specific interfering-factors evidence.
6. Run controls, validity, calculation and reporting rules.
7. Invalid/adverse-result and product-impact pathway.
8. Observed BOM, hands-on, reader and review demand.
9. Reagent/platform continuity and lifecycle governance.
10. Specialist-reviewed estimate-to-actual cases.
