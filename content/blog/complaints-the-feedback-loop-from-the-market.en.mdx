# Complaints: the quality feedback loop from the real world

Your in-house testing can tell you a lot about a batch — but it cannot tell you how
the product behaves in thousands of hands, across real storage and use. **Complaints**
fill that gap. They are the quality system's feedback loop from the real world, and
handled well they catch problems that no release test would.

## Triage comes first — and screens for harm

Not every complaint is equal, so the first step is **triage**: classify by potential
impact and, critically, screen for a **potential adverse event or safety signal**. A
complaint that suggests harm to a patient is on a different, faster track — it may
trigger pharmacovigilance reporting obligations with tight regulatory timelines.
Missing that screen is one of the more serious failures in complaint handling.

## From a single complaint to a real investigation

A genuine quality complaint feeds the same machinery as any other signal:

- **Investigate** the cause where warranted — was it a true product defect, a
  handling issue, or unfounded?
- Assess **batch impact** — does this point at a systemic problem affecting other
  units or batches?
- Drive **CAPA** for confirmed root causes
- Respond to the complainant within defined timelines

## The power is in the trend

One complaint is an anecdote; a **trend** is intelligence. The value of a complaint
system is in aggregating and trending them — by product, defect type, and batch — so a
slow rise in a particular defect surfaces before it becomes a crisis. This is also a
key input to the Annual Product Review.

## The recall link

Complaint handling sits right next to recall management for a reason: a complaint
investigation is often what reveals a defect serious enough to require a **field
action or recall**. A complaint system that detects, investigates, and trends well is
your best early warning against ever needing one.
