# GMP Explained: the Core Principles

Good Manufacturing Practice (GMP) is the system of rules that ensures medicines
are consistently produced and controlled to quality standards. If you're new to
pharma QC/QA, these are the principles everything else builds on.

## The big ideas

- **Quality is built in, not tested in.** You can't inspect quality into a
  product at the end — it comes from a controlled, validated process.
- **Write it down (and follow it).** Approved procedures (SOPs), executed records,
  and good documentation practice make quality provable.
- **Control contamination.** Facilities, gowning, cleaning, and environmental
  monitoring keep the product safe.
- **Validate and qualify.** Equipment, methods, and processes must be proven fit
  for purpose before routine use.
- **Investigate and improve.** Deviations and out-of-specification results are
  investigated to root cause, with CAPA to prevent recurrence.
- **People are qualified.** Training and competency make the system real.

## Why it matters

Every GMP rule traces back to patient safety. A medicine that's contaminated,
under-potent, or made from an uncontrolled process can harm people — so
regulators (FDA, EMA, WHO) enforce GMP through inspections.
