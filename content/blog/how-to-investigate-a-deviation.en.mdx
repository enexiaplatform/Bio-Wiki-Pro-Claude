# How to Investigate a Deviation (Step by Step)

A deviation is any departure from an approved procedure or standard. How you
investigate it — not the fact that it happened — is what an inspector judges.
Here's the workflow.

## 1. Contain and record immediately

Document what happened, when, and what was affected, contemporaneously. Take
immediate **correction** to control the situation (e.g. quarantine product).

## 2. Assess impact

What batches, materials, equipment, or data are affected? Impact assessment
scopes the investigation and drives whether product can be released.

## 3. Find the root cause

Use a structured tool (5 Whys, Ishikawa/fishbone). Push past the symptom — "operator
error" is rarely a root cause; *why* was the error possible?

## 4. Classify

Minor, major, or critical — based on risk to product quality and the patient. The
classification sets the rigor and approval level.

## 5. CAPA

Define **corrective** actions (fix the cause) and **preventive** actions (stop
recurrence), each with an owner, due date, and an **effectiveness check** with
objective acceptance criteria.

## The mistakes auditors flag

- Root cause not established (jumping straight to actions).
- "Human error" with no systemic fix.
- CAPA closed without verifying effectiveness.
- Investigation not contemporaneous / opened late.
