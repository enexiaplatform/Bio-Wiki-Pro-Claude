# Microbiology sample receipt, hold and custody planning

A microbiology result begins before sample preparation. Collection time, container,
transport, receipt status, storage and test-start delay can change whether the tested
material represents the intended decision.

```text
sampling event and decision
→ protected collection and transport
→ receipt, identity and condition checks
→ accession, status and custody
→ controlled storage and test-start queue
→ preparation and method execution
→ exception, rejection or impact decision
```

Atlas can compile the workflow and workload. It does not approve sampling plans,
establish universal hold times, accept a compromised sample or determine whether a
result remains valid after an excursion.

## Define the sample application

Create a record for each product/matrix × sampling purpose × method combination:

- product, material, water point, environmental location or process stage;
- batch, lot, location, time and sampling event;
- release, in-process, monitoring, stability, study or investigation decision;
- approved sampling plan and quantity;
- sample container, closure and headspace;
- preservative or neutralizer where applicable;
- required transport and storage condition;
- time-zero event and maximum test-start condition;
- mixing, subsampling or composite rule;
- hazards and containment;
- method, specification and market applicability;
- final decision owner and escalation route.

The same physical material may require separate records when the decision, sampling
basis or hold condition changes.

## Build the receipt acceptance contract

At receipt, verify and record as applicable:

- sample identifier and request match;
- source batch, point or event;
- collector and collection time;
- container identity, integrity, closure and leakage;
- quantity and fill condition;
- transport temperature or monitor status;
- elapsed time and route;
- label legibility and tamper evidence;
- requested method, priority and due time;
- visible anomaly, damage or contamination;
- accompanying documents and special instructions;
- acceptance, quarantine, conditional acceptance or rejection status.

Do not allow accession to silently convert a damaged or late sample into an accepted
sample. Conditional acceptance needs a reason, decision authority and impact path.

## Separate custody, status and location

The record should answer:

- Who currently controls the sample?
- Where is it physically stored?
- What is its status?
- Which quantity remains?
- Which preparation or transfer has occurred?
- Which method version and request applies?
- When is the next action due?

Possible statuses include expected, received, quarantined, accepted, rejected,
allocated, in preparation, in test, retained, under investigation, returned and
disposed. The site must define its controlled vocabulary and permitted transitions.

## Make hold-time logic explicit

Define:

- the event that starts the clock;
- whether collection, manufacturing completion, receipt or preparation is time zero;
- maximum time to refrigeration, incubation or test start;
- permitted temperature and transport condition;
- qualified container and sample quantity;
- mixing or resuspension before use;
- allowable intermediate holds after preparation;
- excursion assessment and approval owner;
- evidence reference and applicability scope.

Microorganisms may grow, die, attach, clump or become less recoverable during a hold.
One hold-time study should not be extended across matrices or containers without a
controlled rationale.

FDA's official [Laboratory Controls Q&A](https://www.fda.gov/drugs/guidances-drugs/questions-and-answers-current-good-manufacturing-practice-requirements-laboratory-controls)
notes in another laboratory context that extended sample holding should be evaluated
for its effect on analytical performance. Atlas applies the general planning lesson:
hold conditions require application-specific evidence, not an assumed universal
allowance.

## Convert receipt into workload and space

Forecast:

- scheduled and unscheduled arrivals by hour/shift;
- package opening, verification and accession time;
- temperature-monitor review;
- labeling and system entry;
- aliquoting or protected subsampling;
- controlled-room, refrigerator and freezer positions;
- quarantine and rejected-sample positions;
- retain and investigation storage;
- handoff, courier and route time;
- second-person or QA review;
- exception, discrepancy and communication workload;
- container, label, seal and monitoring-device demand.

Average samples per month cannot size a receipt desk when water rounds, EM routes and
batch samples arrive together near a shift cutoff.

## Place samples on a test-start calendar

For each stream, model:

- expected arrival window;
- maximum queue before preparation;
- analyst and equipment availability;
- method batching opportunity;
- media/reagent readiness;
- sample-stability deadline;
- weekend and holiday coverage;
- priority and conflict rule;
- downstream incubation or instrument slot;
- review and release due time.

Batching can improve efficiency only when the approved hold and turnaround permit it.
A low monthly workload can still fail if all samples require immediate start.

## Control splitting, pooling and transfers

Record every material-changing step:

- original quantity and container;
- aliquot, split or composite identifier;
- quantity transferred and remaining;
- receiving container and condition;
- person, time and location;
- method or destination;
- contamination-control precautions;
- reconciliation and disposal.

Do not infer that one retained unit can support every repeat, confirmation and
investigation scenario. Quantity planning should follow approved retest/resample and
retention governance.

## Design the exception pathway

Define actions for:

- late arrival;
- missing or conflicting identity;
- broken, leaking or open container;
- insufficient quantity;
- temperature or transport excursion;
- wrong container or neutralizer;
- unapproved sampler or location;
- unavailable method or media;
- duplicate or cancelled request;
- lost custody or storage alarm;
- suspected cross-contamination.

The pathway should preserve the original observation, protect the sample, notify the
decision owner, assess impact and prevent unauthorized testing or disposal.

## Evidence package before workflow approval

Collect:

1. sampling-purpose and method matrix;
2. approved containers, quantities and transport conditions;
3. hold-time and intermediate-hold evidence;
4. receipt acceptance and rejection criteria;
5. custody, status and location transitions;
6. splitting, pooling and retain rules;
7. arrival and test-start calendar;
8. storage geometry and alarm coverage;
9. exception and impact workflow;
10. external courier or contract-laboratory interfaces;
11. roles, records and system configuration;
12. actual receipt time, queue, rejection and excursion data.

## What Atlas should block

Atlas should not label sample management review-ready when:

- time zero or maximum test-start condition is undefined;
- container, quantity or transport basis is missing;
- receipt checks cannot distinguish accepted from compromised samples;
- status, location and custody are merged in free text;
- storage and quarantine capacity are not modeled;
- peak arrivals and shift cutoffs are hidden by monthly averages;
- splitting and remaining quantity are not reconciled;
- late, damaged or temperature-excursion decisions have no owner;
- external handoffs and courier time are omitted;
- actual queue and exception performance cannot be captured.
