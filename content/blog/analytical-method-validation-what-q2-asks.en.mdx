# Analytical method validation: what ICH Q2 actually asks

Method validation answers one question: **is this method fit for its intended
purpose?** Not "is it a good method in the abstract" — fit for *this* test, on
*this* product, at *this* level. ICH Q2 sets out the performance characteristics
you study to show it.

## The Q2 characteristics

- **Specificity** — can the method measure the analyte unambiguously in the presence
  of everything else (excipients, impurities, degradants)? This is foundational; a
  method that cannot tell the analyte from its degradant is not stability-indicating.
- **Accuracy** — does it give a result close to the true value (typically assessed by
  recovery)?
- **Precision** — repeatability, intermediate precision, and reproducibility: does it
  give consistent results across runs, days, analysts, and instruments?
- **Linearity and range** — is the response proportional to concentration across the
  range you actually test?
- **Detection limit (LOD) and quantitation limit (LOQ)** — for impurity and trace
  methods, how low can you reliably detect and quantify?
- **Robustness** — does the method survive small, deliberate changes (mobile-phase
  composition, temperature, flow) without falling apart?

## The purpose drives which you need

You do not study every characteristic for every method. The **type of test** decides:

- An **assay** needs specificity, accuracy, precision, linearity, and range
- A **quantitative impurity** test adds LOD/LOQ
- A **limit test** for impurities leans on specificity and detection limit
- An **identification** test mainly needs specificity

Matching the validation to the purpose is the craft — over-validating wastes effort,
under-validating leaves a gap an inspector will find.

## The lifecycle view

ICH Q14 frames analytical procedures as a **lifecycle**, like process validation:
define what the method must deliver (the analytical target profile), develop and
validate it, then keep it in control through transfer, system suitability, and
ongoing performance monitoring. Validation is the milestone, not the whole story.
