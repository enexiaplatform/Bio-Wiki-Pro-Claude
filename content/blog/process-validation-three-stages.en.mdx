# Process Validation: the Three-Stage Lifecycle

Process validation moved long ago from "three batches and you're done" to a
**lifecycle**. The FDA's guidance frames it in three stages that span the whole
commercial life of a product.

## Stage 1 — Process Design

Define the commercial process based on development and scale-up knowledge.
Identify critical quality attributes (CQAs) and the critical process parameters
(CPPs) that affect them, often using risk assessment and a control strategy.

## Stage 2 — Process Qualification (PPQ)

Confirm the designed process is reproducible at commercial scale. This includes
qualifying the facility/equipment and running **Process Performance
Qualification** batches under the defined control strategy with heightened
sampling and pre-defined acceptance criteria.

## Stage 3 — Continued Process Verification (CPV)

Validation never really "ends." Routine production data is trended (often with
statistical process control) to confirm the process stays in a state of control —
and to catch drift before it becomes a failure.

## Why the lifecycle matters

The old "validate once" mindset missed slow drift between revalidations. The
lifecycle ties design, qualification, and ongoing monitoring into one continuous
assurance of quality.
