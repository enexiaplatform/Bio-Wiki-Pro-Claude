# 7 Data Integrity Findings That Show Up in FDA 483s

Data integrity dominates modern inspections. The same findings recur across
warning letters — and almost all are preventable with basic ALCOA+ discipline.
Here are seven, with the fix for each.

## 1. Shared logins / generic accounts

Results can't be attributed to a person. **Fix:** unique accounts, individual
credentials, no shared "admin" logins on lab systems.

## 2. Audit trails disabled or not reviewed

An audit trail nobody reviews is decoration. **Fix:** enable audit trails and
make audit-trail review a scheduled, documented activity tied to batch release.

## 3. "Testing into compliance"

Re-running until a passing result appears, discarding the rest. **Fix:** a
pre-approved OOS procedure; every injection/result is accounted for.

## 4. Orphan / unsaved data

Trial injections or deleted files that never made it into the record. **Fix:**
no "test" or unofficial runs; all acquired data is retained and explained.

## 5. Back-dating and pre-signing

Records signed before (or long after) the activity. **Fix:** contemporaneous
recording; signatures reflect the actual time of the work.

## 6. Inadequate access controls

Analysts able to alter methods, clocks, or audit-trail settings. **Fix:**
role-based permissions; only authorized roles change configuration.

## 7. Paper records with uncontrolled corrections

White-out, overwrites, missing reasons for change. **Fix:** single-line cross-out,
initial, date, reason — never obscure the original.

## The pattern

Six of seven are cultural, not technical. Teams that record contemporaneously,
review audit trails, and correct errors openly rarely generate serious findings —
the systems just make those habits enforceable.
