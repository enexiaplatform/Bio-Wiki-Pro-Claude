# Part 11: what makes an electronic record trustworthy

When QC data lives in software rather than on paper, a question follows: can you
**trust** it the way you trusted a signed logbook? **21 CFR Part 11** in the US (and
**EU GMP Annex 11**) answer that — they define the conditions under which an electronic
record and an electronic signature are as reliable, attributable, and durable as their
paper equivalents.

## The controls that make a record trustworthy

Part 11 / Annex 11 are really a checklist of what it takes to trust electronic data:

- **Audit trails** — secure, computer-generated, time-stamped records of who did what
  and when, that **users cannot alter or disable**. This is the heart of it.
- **Access control** — unique user accounts, role-based permissions, and no shared
  logins, so every action is **attributable**.
- **Electronic signatures** — linked to the record, with the signer's name, the
  date/time, and the meaning of the signature, and bound so they cannot be copied to
  another record.
- **Accurate copies and retention** — you can produce complete, readable copies and
  retain records for their required lifetime.
- **System validation** — the software is validated to perform reliably and to enforce
  these controls.

## The shift to review-by-exception

The practical pivot Part 11 enables — and demands — is **reviewing the audit trail**.
An electronic record is only trustworthy if someone actually reviews the exceptions:
the changes, the aborted runs, the re-integrations. Signing off the result without
reviewing the audit trail is the modern equivalent of signing a logbook page you never
read.

## Part 11 is a means, not the end

A common trap is treating Part 11 compliance as the goal. It is not — **data integrity**
is the goal, and Part 11 is one set of controls that supports it for electronic systems.
A perfectly Part 11-configured system used by people who do not review audit trails or
who share an admin login still has a data-integrity problem. The technology enables
trust; the people and the quality culture deliver it.
