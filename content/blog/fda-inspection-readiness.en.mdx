# FDA Inspection Readiness: a QC team's checklist

Inspection readiness is not a project you run the week before an FDA visit — it
is the state your quality system lives in. Teams that treat it as routine rarely
scramble.

## Be ready every day

- **Documents retrievable in minutes** — batch records, SOPs, training records,
  calibration logs, and CAPA files.
- **Audit trails reviewed on schedule** — not reconstructed on demand.
- **Open investigations on track** — no overdue deviations or CAPAs aging
  silently past their due dates.

## The front room / back room model

A **front room** hosts the inspector and answers questions; a **back room**
retrieves documents, vets them, and tracks every request. Nothing reaches the
inspector without a quick QA check for completeness and relevance.

## The four themes behind most 483s

1. Data integrity gaps (audit trails, shared logins).
2. Inadequate investigations (root cause not established).
3. CAPA without effectiveness verification.
4. Laboratory controls — OOS handling and method validation.

## Handling requests well

Answer the question asked — no more, no less. If you don't know, say you'll find
out; never guess. Keep a running log of every document provided so your responses
stay consistent across days.

A team that records contemporaneously, closes investigations on time, and reviews
its own audit trails has already done 90% of inspection prep.
