# CSV vs CSA: validating computerised systems without the paper mountain

Computerised systems that handle GMP data (LIMS, chromatography software, MES)
must be validated. The classic approach is **CSV** (GAMP 5); the newer **CSA**
(Computer Software Assurance) reframes it around risk and critical thinking.

## GAMP 5 categories

Effort scales with the software category — from standard infrastructure (low
risk) to custom/bespoke applications (high risk). Don't validate a spreadsheet
like a bespoke MES.

## The CSA shift

CSA (FDA draft guidance) emphasises **critical thinking and risk** over
exhaustive scripted testing and documentation for its own sake. More unscripted
/ exploratory testing for low-risk features; rigorous scripted testing where
patient impact is real.

## What still matters

Whatever you call it: a documented risk assessment, requirements traceability,
testing proportional to risk, and **data integrity controls** (audit trails,
access, backups). The label changed; the assurance objective didn't.
