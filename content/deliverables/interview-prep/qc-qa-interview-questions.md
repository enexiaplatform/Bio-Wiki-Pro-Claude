# 100+ QC/QA Interview Questions

Grouped by theme. The starred (★) questions are the ones that most often decide
the outcome — prepare a real STAR example for each.

> **STAR:** Situation → Task → Action → Result. Keep it to ~90 seconds, end on a
> quantified result, and make every detail true.

## How to answer the 3 questions that most often decide it

**"How would you handle an OOS?"** — Show you know the *process*, not just the
chemistry: confirm the data, Phase I lab assessment, invalidate only on a
documented assignable cause, Phase II full-scale if none, and never retest into
compliance. Calm and procedural wins.

**"Have you been pressured to pass something?"** — They're testing integrity. The
only safe answer is honest: quality decisions are independent of production; if
pressured, you escalate through QA and document. If it never happened, say so
plainly.

**"Tell me about a serious problem you found."** — Pick a real one you handled
well. STAR it, and make the *Result* show the system worked: root cause found, fix
verified, recurrence prevented. This single answer often carries the interview.
