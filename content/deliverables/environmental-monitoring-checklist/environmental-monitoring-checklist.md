# Environmental Monitoring Program Checklist

General educational workflow — verify against the applicable regulations
(e.g. Annex 1, USP), your approved SOPs, and your validated quality system.
Not a substitute for QA approval or a validated method.

---

## 1. Program design (risk-based)

- [ ] EM program tied to the contamination control strategy (CCS)
- [ ] Sample locations selected from a documented risk assessment (worst-case, not convenient)
- [ ] Grades (A-D) mapped to methods: active air, settle plates, contact/swab, personnel
- [ ] Alert and action limits set from guidance plus historical data
- [ ] Trending method and review frequency defined

## 2. Methods and frequency

- [ ] Active-air sample volume defined per grade
- [ ] Settle-plate exposure time defined and justified
- [ ] Contact/swab locations and surfaces defined
- [ ] Personnel monitoring defined for every grade A/B aseptic operation
- [ ] Sampling frequency justified by risk and process

## 3. Media and incubation

- [ ] Media qualified by growth promotion and within expiry
- [ ] Incubation regime recovers both bacteria and fungi (e.g. dual-temperature)
- [ ] Plate reconciliation procedure in place (every plate accounted for)

## 4. Sampling execution

- [ ] Operators trained and qualified in aseptic sampling technique
- [ ] The sampling intervention itself is controlled (no secondary contamination)
- [ ] Samples protected from contamination in transit to the lab

## 5. Data, trending and limits

- [ ] Counts plotted against alert and action limits
- [ ] Per-site AND aggregate trends reviewed
- [ ] Adverse trends below the action limit are investigated, not ignored
- [ ] Action-limit recoveries identified to genus/species

## 6. Excursion response

- [ ] Defined timeline to open an excursion investigation
- [ ] Product impact assessed for each action-limit breach
- [ ] Root cause established and CAPA driven
- [ ] Recurring-site excursions trigger an airflow/facility review

## 7. Audit readiness

- [ ] EM trends summarized and current
- [ ] Excursion investigations closed with effective CAPA
- [ ] Site-selection rationale documented and defensible
- [ ] Personnel monitoring records complete for aseptic operations
- [ ] Limits and methods consistent with the registered/validated state
