# Cover Letter Templates (QC/QA)

Three adaptable versions. Keep to ~250 words. Replace every [bracket]. Always name
the company and one specific reason you want *this* role.

---

## 1. Senior QC/QA (experienced)

Dear [Hiring Manager],

I'm applying for the [role] at [Company]. With [X] years in QC/QA for [pharma /
sterile / biologics], I've built a track record of [headline result — e.g.
"audit-ready labs and investigations that hold up under inspection"].

At [current company] I [concrete achievement with a number]. I work fluently to
[GMP WHO / EU Annex 1 / ICH], own [OOS, CAPA, validation], and care about data
integrity as a daily discipline, not a slogan.

[Company] stands out to me because [specific, genuine reason]. I'd bring [the
specific value], and I'd welcome the chance to discuss how.

Sincerely, [Name]

---

## 2. Entry / early-career

Dear [Hiring Manager],

I'm excited to apply for the [role] at [Company]. My [degree] in [field] and
hands-on experience with [techniques/instruments] have given me a solid grounding
in [GMP, aseptic technique, analytical testing].

During [project/internship] I [specific accomplishment]. I learn procedures fast,
document carefully, and understand why good documentation practice matters in a
regulated lab.

I'd be proud to start my QC/QA career at [Company] because [reason]. Thank you for
considering my application.

Sincerely, [Name]

---

## 3. Career-changer (into QC/QA)

Dear [Hiring Manager],

I'm writing to apply for the [role] at [Company]. While my background is in
[previous field], my experience with [transferable skills — precision,
documentation, lab work, compliance] maps directly to QC/QA, and I've deepened my
GMP knowledge through [course/self-study/this platform].

In my current role I [transferable achievement with a number]. I'm drawn to QC/QA
because [genuine motivation], and to [Company] specifically because [reason].

I'd value the opportunity to show how my perspective strengthens your team.

Sincerely, [Name]

---

**Tip:** the worst cover letter restates the CV. The best one connects *your*
specific evidence to *their* specific need — and proves you researched the company.
