# LinkedIn Profile Guide for QC/QA Professionals

Recruiters search LinkedIn with keywords. This guide gets your profile found and
makes it convert once they land.

## Headline (the most important field)
Don't just put your job title. Use: **[Role] | [specialty] | [standards]**.
- ✅ "QA Specialist | Sterile Manufacturing | EU Annex 1 & Data Integrity"
- ✅ "QC Analyst | HPLC Method Validation | ICH Q2"
- ❌ "QC Analyst at [Company]"

## About (3 short paragraphs)
1. Who you are + years + domain (pharma/biologics/API/F&B).
2. What you're strong at — name the standards and the outcomes (quantified).
3. What you're looking for / open to, and a line of personality.

Write in first person. Front-load keywords recruiters search: GMP, validation,
OOS, CAPA, data integrity, Annex 1, ICH, LIMS, environmental monitoring.

## Experience
Mirror your CV bullets — verb + action + number. LinkedIn is also keyword-indexed,
so don't abbreviate the standards.

## Skills (pick the ones recruiters filter on)
Good Manufacturing Practice (GMP) · Quality Assurance · Quality Control · Method
Validation · CAPA · Deviation Management · Data Integrity · HPLC · Environmental
Monitoring · Internal Auditing · Stability Testing · Regulatory Compliance

Get a few colleagues to endorse the top 3 — endorsed skills rank higher in search.

## Settings that matter
- Turn on **"Open to work"** (recruiters-only mode if discreet).
- Set your location to where you'll actually work (location is a hard filter).
- Custom URL (linkedin.com/in/yourname) — put it on your CV.

## The 20-minute weekly habit
Comment thoughtfully on 2–3 posts in your field each week. Visibility compounds —
recruiters notice active, knowledgeable professionals long before they post a job.
