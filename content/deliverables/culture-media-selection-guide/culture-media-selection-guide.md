# Culture Media Selection Guide

General educational workflow — verify against the applicable pharmacopeia
(USP/EP/JP), your approved SOPs, and your validated quality system. Not a
substitute for QA approval or a validated method.

---

## 1. Match the media to the method

Start from the compendial chapter for the test (e.g. USP 61/62, USP 71,
EP 2.6.x). Use the named medium or a justified equivalent, and decide whether you
need a non-selective or a selective/differential medium:

- **Non-selective** (recovery of total counts, sterility): grows a broad range of
  organisms.
- **Selective / differential** (screening for specified or objectionable
  organisms): recovers the target while suppressing or distinguishing others.

## 2. Common media by purpose

- Total aerobic microbial count / sterility: Tryptic Soy Agar (TSA / SCDA / CASO)
- Yeasts and moulds: Sabouraud Dextrose Agar (SDA)
- Gram-negative / bile-tolerant organisms: MacConkey Agar, Violet Red Bile Agar
- Pseudomonas aeruginosa: Cetrimide Agar
- Staphylococcus aureus: Mannitol Salt Agar or Baird-Parker Agar
- Enrichment / pre-incubation: Tryptic Soy Broth (TSB / SCDB)

Always confirm the specific medium named (or its justified equivalent) in your
method — this list is a starting point, not a substitute for the monograph.

## 3. Growth promotion testing (GPT)

- [ ] Run GPT against the compendial strains before release-testing use
- [ ] For selective media, demonstrate BOTH recovery of the target and inhibition of non-target organisms
- [ ] Use a low inoculum (typically not more than 100 CFU) to challenge recovery
- [ ] Record the strain, lot, and result

## 4. Preparation and sterilization

- [ ] Follow the manufacturer's instructions for hydration and sterilization
- [ ] Measure and record pH after sterilization (within the method range)
- [ ] Avoid over-autoclaving — it degrades selective agents and harms recovery of fastidious organisms
- [ ] Record lot, expiry, and every preparation parameter

## 5. Equivalence and substitution

- [ ] Any substitution for a named medium is supported by a documented equivalence rationale
- [ ] The change is assessed and approved through change control

## 6. Storage and expiry

- [ ] Store at the labeled conditions; protect from light and dehydration
- [ ] Apply a use-by date and quarantine the lot until GPT passes
- [ ] Link the media lot to the tests it supports for full traceability
