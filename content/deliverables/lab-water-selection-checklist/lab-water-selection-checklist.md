# Lab Water Selection Checklist

General educational workflow — verify against the applicable pharmacopeia
(USP/EP/JP), your approved SOPs, and your validated water system. Not a substitute
for QA approval or a validated method.

---

## 1. Define the use

- [ ] Application identified (parenteral, sterile non-injectable, non-sterile, analytical, cleaning)
- [ ] Product-contact vs non-contact step distinguished
- [ ] Route and dosage form considered for sterile products

## 2. Select the grade

- [ ] Grade chosen for the use: Water for Injection (injectable product-contact), Purified Water (non-sterile / cleaning), reagent-grade (analytical)
- [ ] Choice justified against the pharmacopeia and the registered specification
- [ ] Initial cleaning may use a lower grade, but the final rinse grade matches the product's needs

## 3. Specifications and monitoring

- [ ] Conductivity and TOC limits defined for the grade
- [ ] Microbial alert and action limits defined
- [ ] Endotoxin limit defined where required (WFI: not more than 0.25 EU/mL)
- [ ] Sampling locations, frequency, and trending plan in place

## 4. System and controls

- [ ] Water drawn from a qualified, validated water system
- [ ] Point-of-use controls defined (sanitization, flushing, hold times)
- [ ] Out-of-limit / out-of-trend response procedure in place

## 5. Documentation

- [ ] Grade-selection rationale documented and defensible
- [ ] Specs and monitoring consistent with the registered/validated state
