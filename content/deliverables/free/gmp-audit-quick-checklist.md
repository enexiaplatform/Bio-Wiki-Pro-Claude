# GMP Audit Quick Checklist (Free)

A one-page readiness scan for your next GMP inspection. Score each line
Yes / Partial / No. Anything not "Yes" is where to focus first.

## 1. Documentation & data integrity
- [ ] Current and superseded SOPs retrievable within minutes.
- [ ] Unique logins on all GxP systems — no shared accounts.
- [ ] Audit trails ON and reviewed, with the review recorded.
- [ ] No one who generates data can delete it or disable its audit trail.
- [ ] Records complete and contemporaneous (no blanks, no late entries).

## 2. Investigations & CAPA
- [ ] OOS handled by a two-phase procedure (lab, then full-scale).
- [ ] No retesting without documented, pre-defined justification.
- [ ] CAPAs have verified root causes (not "retrained the operator").
- [ ] Every CAPA has an effectiveness check with objective data.
- [ ] Deviations risk-assessed and closed with an impact statement.

## 3. Validation & equipment
- [ ] Equipment qualified (IQ/OQ/PQ) and within calibration.
- [ ] Cleaning limits HBEL-based, with a recovery study on file.
- [ ] Analytical methods validated to ICH Q2 for their purpose.
- [ ] Computer systems validated for intended use (risk-based).

## 4. Sterility / micro (if applicable)
- [ ] A current, living Contamination Control Strategy (Annex 1).
- [ ] EM excursions identified to species and impact-assessed.
- [ ] Media fills designed to a zero-contamination expectation.
- [ ] Gowning qualification current for all cleanroom personnel.

## 5. Quality system
- [ ] Changes assessed and approved before implementation.
- [ ] Critical suppliers have quality agreements + change notification.
- [ ] Training records match who actually performed the work.
- [ ] Self-inspection program is risk-based and findings drive CAPA.
- [ ] Previous inspection commitments all verifiably closed.

## 6. Audit-day readiness
- [ ] Front room / back room set; runners and a scribe assigned.
- [ ] Team rehearsed: answer the question asked, truthfully, then stop.
- [ ] Any document retrievable in minutes.

---

**Score:** count your "Yes" answers. Below ~90%? Prioritize the gaps in sections
1 and 2 first — data integrity and investigations are where most 483s are written.
