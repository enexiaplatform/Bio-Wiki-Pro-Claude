# OOS Investigation Template — Start Here

A ready-to-fill template for running an out-of-specification (OOS) laboratory
investigation that holds up to inspection. It follows the phased approach in the
FDA OOS guidance: a Phase I laboratory assessment, then a Phase II full-scale
investigation when no assignable laboratory cause is found, and a QA-owned
disposition.

## What's inside

1. **OOS Investigation Template (PDF)** — the full phased form: triggering result,
   Phase I laboratory assessment, hypothesis testing, Phase II production
   investigation, disposition, and CAPA. Print it or copy it into your QMS.
2. **OOS Investigation Log (CSV/Excel)** — a step-by-step checklist log with
   owner, status, and timestamp columns for tracking the investigation.

## How to use it

1. Open the template the moment a confirmed OOS is reported — **before** any
   solution is discarded.
2. Work top to bottom. Do not move to retest/re-prepare without a documented,
   QA-approved hypothesis.
3. If Phase I finds no assignable laboratory cause, escalate to Phase II — do not
   invalidate the result.
4. QA, independent of the analyst, records the final batch disposition and
   rationale.

## Important

General educational workflow. This template is a starting point — verify it
against your approved OOS SOP, the applicable regulations, and your validated
quality system. It is not a substitute for QA approval or a validated method.
