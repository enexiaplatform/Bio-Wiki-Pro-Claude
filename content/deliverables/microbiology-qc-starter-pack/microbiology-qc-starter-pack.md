# Microbiology QC Starter Guide

General educational workflow — verify against the applicable pharmacopeia
(USP/EP/JP), your approved SOPs, and your validated quality system. Not a substitute
for QA approval, formal training, or a validated method.

---

## 1. The core QC microbiology tests

- **Sterility testing (USP 71)** — confirms a product is free of viable
  microorganisms; aseptic technique and negative controls are everything.
- **Bioburden / microbial limits (USP 61/62)** — total counts and screening for
  specified/objectionable organisms in non-sterile products.
- **Environmental monitoring** — risk-based monitoring of cleanrooms and personnel
  to show the contamination control strategy is working.
- **Endotoxin / LAL testing** — limits bacterial endotoxin in parenteral products.
- **Water testing** — microbial and chemical control of pharmaceutical waters.
- **Growth promotion / media control** — every media lot qualified before use.

## 2. What good looks like (key controls)

- [ ] Every media lot qualified by growth promotion before use
- [ ] Aseptic technique controlled; positive/negative controls run with each test
- [ ] Results trended over time, not just judged pass/fail each run
- [ ] Excursions investigated and identified to genus/species
- [ ] Everything traceable: media lot, analyst, method, equipment

## 3. Common early mistakes

- Using media with no growth promotion test or past expiry
- Disturbing the aseptic field while sampling
- Treating an alert limit as a "pass" and ignoring the trend
- Not identifying organisms recovered at the action limit

## 4. Your first 90 days

Use the **First 90 Days Checklist** (the CSV in this pack) with your supervisor.
It covers reading the fundamentals, shadowing each core test, getting qualified on
aseptic technique and gowning, and your first supervised runs.
