# Audit Interview Q&A Scripts

50+ questions GMP auditors ask most, with model answers for QC/QA. Rehearse out
loud with your team. The golden rule under every answer: **answer the question
asked, truthfully, then stop.**

## A. Data integrity
1. *"Walk me through how you review audit trails."* — Describe the SOP, who
   reviews (independent of the analyst), frequency by risk, and where the review is
   recorded. Then show one.
2. *"Can two people use the same login?"* — "No. Every user has a unique account;
   shared logins are prohibited by our access SOP."
3. *"Who can delete data on this system?"* — "No one who generates data. Delete and
   audit-trail configuration are restricted to system administrators in IT."
4. *"Show me a reprocessed chromatogram and why it was reprocessed."* — Retrieve
   it, explain the documented justification and approval.
5. *"How do you know the audit trail can't be turned off?"* — Show the configuration
   and the change-control around it.

## B. OOS / investigations
6. *"Take me through your last OOS."* — Phase I lab assessment, Phase II if no lab
   cause, the disposition. Stress no retesting without justification.
7. *"When can you invalidate a result?"* — "Only with a documented, assignable lab
   error confirmed in Phase I."
8. *"How many retests are allowed?"* — "Retests are pre-defined in the SOP with
   scientific justification; we do not test into compliance."
9. *"What happens to other batches when an OOS is confirmed?"* — Describe the
   scope/impact assessment.

## C. Deviations & CAPA
10. *"How do you decide a deviation needs a CAPA?"* — Risk- and trend-based triage.
11. *"Show me a CAPA you closed and how you knew it worked."* — Show the
    effectiveness check with objective data.
12. *"Why isn't 'retrain the operator' your root cause here?"* — Explain you look
    for the systemic cause that let the error happen and go undetected.
13. *"How do you trend deviations?"* — Describe the metric, review forum, and
    escalation.

## D. Change control
14. *"How are changes approved?"* — Assessed for impact and approved before
    implementation; show the log.
15. *"Show me a change made in the last 3 months."* — Retrieve it; walk the
    assessment.
16. *"What changes don't go through change control?"* — Be precise; like-for-like
    within defined limits, documented.

## E. Cleaning validation
17. *"What's the basis for your cleaning limits?"* — HBEL/PDE-derived MACO.
18. *"What's your recovery factor and how was it established?"* — Coupon recovery
    study; results corrected for it.
19. *"What's your worst-case product and why?"* — Hardest to clean / most potent.
20. *"How do you handle dirty and clean hold times?"* — Both validated with data.

## F. Environmental monitoring (sterile/non-sterile)
21. *"What are your alert and action levels and how were they set?"* — Statistical/
    historical basis.
22. *"Show me your last action-level excursion."* — ID, trend, impact assessment,
    CAPA.
23. *"What's your normal flora?"* — Reference your site flora library.
24. *"How do you investigate a Grade A excursion?"* — Walk the decision tree.

## G. Validation & qualification
25. *"Is this equipment qualified and calibrated?"* — Show IQ/OQ/PQ + calibration
    status.
26. *"What's the difference between qualification and validation here?"* — Equipment
    is qualified; processes/methods are validated.
27. *"Walk me through your process validation lifecycle."* — Stage 1 design, Stage 2
    PPQ, Stage 3 CPV.
28. *"How is this method validated?"* — ICH Q2 characteristics relevant to the test.

## H. Stability
29. *"How many batches go on stability per year?"* — At least one per product/
    strength/pack made.
30. *"What if a stability result fails?"* — Assess marketed batches; possible field
    action.

## I. Supplier control
31. *"How do you qualify suppliers?"* — Risk tiers; audits/agreements for critical.
32. *"Does this supplier notify you of changes?"* — Point to the agreement clause.

## J. Training & personnel
33. *"Is this operator trained for this task?"* — Show the record dated before the
    work.
34. *"Who qualified the trainer?"* — Show the trainer qualification.

## K. Documentation / GDP
35. *"Why is this entry late / corrected this way?"* — Explain the deviation; show
    single-line correction, initialed and dated.
36. *"Show me the current and the previous version of this SOP."* — Retrieve both
    from document control.

## L. Behavioral / pressure questions
37. *"Has anyone ever asked you to change a result?"* — Truthful, calm: "No," and if
    yes, describe how it was reported.
38. *"What would you do if you found a problem after release?"* — Notify QA; assess;
    deviation/CAPA; field action if needed.
39. *"What's the worst quality problem you've had here?"* — Pick a real one you
    handled well; show the system worked.
40. *"Do you feel pressured by production timelines?"* — Quality decisions are
    independent of production; explain the structure.

## M. Rapid-fire (have crisp answers ready)
41. Who is your Qualified Person / who releases batches?
42. Where is your quality manual / site master file?
43. How do you handle a complaint?
44. What's your recall procedure and when did you last test it?
45. How are computer systems validated (GAMP categories)?
46. What is your data backup and restore process?
47. How do you control reference standards?
48. What's your gowning qualification process?
49. How do you investigate a media-fill failure?
50. What's your self-inspection schedule and who performs it?
51. How do management-review outputs drive resources?
52. Where would I see your quality metrics / KPIs?

> Practice tip: the goal isn't scripted answers — it's calm, honest, specific ones
> backed by a record you can produce in minutes. Run a mock interview where a
> colleague plays a skeptical auditor.
