# 10 CAPA Report Templates

Ready-to-fill CAPA structures for the nonconformances GMP auditors raise most.
Copy the block, fill every field, and never close without an effectiveness check.

## The universal CAPA structure (use for all 10)

```
CAPA ID:                      Date opened:            Owner:
Source: (audit / deviation / OOS / complaint / trend)
1. Problem statement (what, where, when, how detected, magnitude):
2. Immediate correction / containment:
3. Risk assessment (severity, scope, other batches/products affected):
4. Root cause method (5 Whys / Ishikawa) and VERIFIED root cause:
5. Corrective action(s) — each mapped to a cause | owner | due date:
6. Preventive action(s) — extend to similar processes/products:
7. Effectiveness check — metric | target | time window | owner:
8. Closure approval (name/date) + effectiveness verification (name/date):
```

Below: the typical root causes and effectiveness checks for each common finding.

---

### 1. Data integrity — unreviewed audit trails
- **Likely root cause:** no defined audit-trail-review SOP / no independence.
- **Corrective:** implement review SOP; review backlog by risk.
- **Preventive:** roll review requirement across all GxP systems.
- **Effectiveness check:** 100% of high-risk batches have a recorded audit-trail
  review over the next quarter.

### 2. OOS investigation — superficial / "testing into compliance"
- **Root cause:** no two-phase OOS procedure; retest without justification.
- **Corrective:** revise OOS SOP (Phase I lab / Phase II full-scale).
- **Effectiveness check:** zero OOS closed via unjustified retest in next 20 cases
  (verified at QA review).

### 3. CAPA ineffective — recurrence
- **Root cause:** action addressed symptom, not cause; no effectiveness check.
- **Corrective:** reopen; re-investigate true root cause.
- **Effectiveness check:** zero recurrence of the same event over a defined window.

### 4. Cleaning validation — no HBEL basis / no recovery study
- **Root cause:** limits set on legacy 10-ppm rule; recovery never established.
- **Corrective:** derive HBEL/PDE-based MACO; run recovery studies; revalidate.
- **Effectiveness check:** all shared-equipment limits HBEL-based and met for 3
  consecutive cleans.

### 5. Environmental monitoring — excursion closed without ID/impact
- **Root cause:** no requirement to identify isolate / assess product impact.
- **Corrective:** revise EM SOP; identify isolates to species; impact template.
- **Effectiveness check:** 100% of action-level excursions in next quarter have ID
  + documented impact assessment.

### 6. Calibration / equipment — overdue or used after failed check
- **Root cause:** no interlock between calibration status and use.
- **Corrective:** status labels; out-of-tolerance impact procedure.
- **Effectiveness check:** zero use of out-of-calibration equipment over 6 months.

### 7. Training — records don't match who performed the work
- **Root cause:** training matrix not tied to task authorization.
- **Corrective:** reconcile matrix; block unqualified sign-offs.
- **Effectiveness check:** training-record audit of 20 batch records shows 100%
  match.

### 8. Change control — change made before approval
- **Root cause:** unclear what constitutes a change; weak gatekeeping.
- **Corrective:** revise change SOP; train; add pre-implementation QA gate.
- **Effectiveness check:** zero unapproved changes in next quarterly review.

### 9. Supplier control — no quality agreement / no change notification
- **Root cause:** suppliers approved without agreements; no notification clause.
- **Corrective:** execute quality agreements; add change-notification clause.
- **Effectiveness check:** 100% of critical suppliers have current agreements.

### 10. Documentation — incomplete / late entries
- **Root cause:** GDP not enforced; forms not contemporaneous.
- **Corrective:** GDP retraining; review-by-second-person at point of entry.
- **Effectiveness check:** documentation-practice audit shows zero critical GDP
  errors over the next 25 records.

> Reminder: if your corrective action is "retrain the analyst," you have almost
> certainly not found the real root cause. Ask what in the *system* allowed the
> error and let it go undetected.
